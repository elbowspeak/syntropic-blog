## Infrastructure Assessment

Grid-scale storage deployment is accelerating on hard numbers, not projections: the US posted its strongest-ever Q1 (9.7 GWh) and is tracking toward 24 GW for 2026, while global capacity growth is running at 36%+ YoY. LFP has effectively won the chemistry war for stationary storage (~95% of new awards), which simplifies supply chain risk assessment to lithium/iron/phosphate rather than the broader critical minerals basket. The real bottleneck is cost geography, not technology — a 2-3.5x cost gap between Chinese and US turnkey systems reflects site, labor, and domestic-content friction more than cell economics, and federal ITC support (30-70%) is what's currently absorbing that gap for US developers.

The more consequential shift this week is in long-duration storage: Form Energy's iron-air system clearing a full year of continuous grid operation, paired with Ore Energy's independent 1 GWh Dutch order, moves multi-day storage from pilot-stage rhetoric to commercial reality with two credible vendors. Solid-state progress remains firmly upstream — a dendrite-cracking fix is a meaningful lab result but does not change near-term deployment math; commercial SSB volume is still an early-2030s story.
