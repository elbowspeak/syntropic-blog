**The LDES segment is the most execution-challenged.** The gap between CPUC's 1,000 MW procurement mandate and the actual installed base of non-lithium long-duration storage is wide. The 2030 projection of 850 global LDES installations requires a permitting and finance velocity that has not yet been demonstrated.

**Grid modernization investment is beginning to materialize in DERMS and VPP infrastructure**, but the NERC 224 GW demand revision underscores that software-layer solutions (VPPs, demand response) are bridging tools, not substitutes for physical generation and storage capacity. The Maryland DERMS reporting requirement (first report due October 2026) and utility DERMS investment announcements signal regulatory infrastructure is catching up to technical capability.
