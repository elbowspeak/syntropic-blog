## Weekly Digest

This week's most compelling find is the BEDS framework, which formally bridges Bayesian inference with dissipative structures under energy constraints — a direct synthesis of Friston's free energy principle with Prigogine-style thermodynamics that deserves close attention. The "Entropy Bathtub" paper offers a Markovian treatment of living systems as dissipative, far-from-equilibrium entities, connecting nicely to Jeremy England's work on thermodynamic foundations of biology. On the Active Inference front, the reformulation of Expected Free Energy into four unified formulations provides useful theoretical clarity for anyone building on Friston's framework. The cognitive fatigue paper, while applied, offers an interesting angle on schema rigidity and entropy externalization that touches on predictive processing and mental energy in ways relevant to philosophy of mind.
