Expect the first commercially significant case study (with disclosed revenue or deployment scale) to come from one of these domains within 18–24 months, likely from a non-VERSES entity.

The most likely scenario is that a large robotics or autonomous systems company (with existing FEP adjacency) funds or acquires a tooling effort rather than a standalone startup raising from traditional VCs. **4. AI governance positioning by the Active Inference Institute will prove strategically important if neurobiologically-grounded AI safety frameworks gain regulatory traction.**

If regulatory frameworks for AI begin to require interpretability standards or uncertainty quantification properties — areas where FEP-based agents have architectural advantages over black-box models — the Institute's presence in those conversations could accelerate enterprise adoption through compliance pathways rather than performance pathways. This is a low-probability, high-impact scenario operating on a 3–5 year horizon.

**5. The "inference" naming collision will worsen before it improves.**
As LLM inference infrastructure continues to attract massive capital (Inferact, Baseten, and likely successor companies), the term "Active Inference" will face increasing communicational friction in investor and enterprise contexts. Expect ecosystem participants to experiment with alternative framings — "generative world models," "Bayesian autonomous agents," "uncertainty-aware AI" — as positioning strategies.
