Causal AI is transitioning from **late early adopter** to **early majority** — but the transition is uneven across verticals and use cases, and that unevenness is itself the most important strategic fact this week.

**What's already crossing the chasm:**

- Causal fairness as a compliance product is at the **critical inflection point** created by the US AI Accountability Act. This is the segment most likely to see rapid commercial development in the next 12 months.

**What's shifting most meaningfully:**
The most significant structural shift this week is the **regulatory normalization of causal accountability requirements**. When compliance teams — not just data science teams — begin driving causal AI procurement, the adoption curve steepens dramatically and the sales motion changes. Causal AI stops being sold into innovation budgets and starts being purchased from compliance and legal budgets, which are larger, stickier, and less sensitive to proof-of-concept timelines.

The secondary shift is the **LLM integration pattern**. causaLens's architecture — causal reasoning as a control layer on LLM outputs rather than a replacement for them — is likely the correct long-term design. It sidesteps the "LLMs will get smarter and eliminate the need for causal AI" objection by making causal structure the *verifiability layer* that makes LLM outputs trustworthy in high-stakes decisions.
