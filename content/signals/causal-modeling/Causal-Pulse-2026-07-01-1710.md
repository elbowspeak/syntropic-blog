## Market Position

**Where causal AI sits on the adoption curve: late early majority, with an infrastructure gap.**

The signals this week collectively point to a field that has moved past proof-of-concept but has not yet achieved commodity infrastructure status. The clearest structural indicator is the simultaneous presence of:

1. **Named enterprise customers in production** (causaLens with J&J, Syneos, GE Vernova; Causaly with 12 top-20 pharma companies) — this is not pilot activity
2. **Active methodological competition** (DeepCausalMMM vs. PyMC-Marketing vs. CausalMMM) — the tooling layer is not settled
3. **Frontier academic output with commercial pressure signatures** (Bareinboim lab cadence, topics focused on production-relevant problems like transfer gaps and Markov equivalence ambiguity)

**What is shifting**: The most important structural shift visible in this week's data is the move from *causal inference as a data science method* to *causal reasoning as an agent capability*. causaLens's "Digital Workers" framing, Ineffable Intelligence's RL-plus-causality mandate, and the LLM4Causal/Causal Agent tooling wrappers all point to the same transition: causal reasoning is being embedded into systems that act, not just systems that report.

This matters commercially because it changes the buyer. A causal inference platform sold to a data science team competes on methodology and tooling. A causal agent platform sold to a business function competes on workflow integration and decision reliability. The latter is a larger market with higher switching costs and more defensible margins — but it requires the vendor to own the intervention loop, not just the analysis.

**The gap that remains**: The data shows no evidence of a dominant horizontal causal AI infrastructure layer analogous to what Snowflake did for data warehousing or what dbt did for data transformation. DoWhy/PyWhy is the closest candidate, but its governance migration to independent control and AWS collaboration, while positive for longevity, has not yet produced the enterprise distribution partnerships that would make it infrastructure rather than tooling. This gap is the primary commercial opportunity that is not yet captured by any player in the dataset.
