**Where causal AI sits on the adoption curve: Late Early Adopter → Early Majority transition, but unevenly distributed by vertical.**

The aggregate picture from this week's signals is of a technology that has cleared proof-of-concept in multiple domains simultaneously and is now navigating the operational scaling challenge — not the scientific legitimacy challenge. Three structural shifts are underway:

**1. From tool to infrastructure.** causaLens's Digital Worker Factory and its IBM partnership reflect a deliberate architectural move: causal reasoning is being embedded *inside* agentic systems as a reliability layer, not offered as a standalone analytical product. This is how transformative technologies achieve durable penetration — they become invisible infrastructure rather than visible features. The implication is that standalone causal inference platforms face a squeeze: they must either integrate upward into agentic workflows or downward into data infrastructure to avoid being commoditized by open-source (PyWhy) from below and AI platform giants (IBM, Google, Microsoft) from above.

**2. Domain stratification is sharpening.** Pharma/biotech is the vertically advanced frontier — Aitia's IND pipeline, Causaly's Pipeline Graph, BERG's Interrogative Biology platform, and the 60%+ trial failure rate attributed to correlative modeling create a concentrated, high-value beachhead where causal AI can charge premium prices and build deep moats. Marketing (causal MMM) is in rapid mid-market adoption driven by regulatory pressure (cookie deprecation) rather than conviction. Supply chain and public sector are early and fragmented, representing the next wave but requiring more systems integration work.

**3. The explainability mandate is becoming a procurement criterion.** As AI governance frameworks mature globally, the ability to answer "why did this model recommend that intervention?" is shifting from a differentiator to a minimum requirement in regulated industries. Causal AI is structurally better positioned to meet this requirement than black-box predictive systems — not because of marketing claims but because of the mathematical properties of DAG-based inference and do-calculus. This gives causal AI vendors a regulatory tailwind that is largely unpriced in the current market narrative, which remains dominated by generative AI hype.

**Key tension to watch:** The massive concentration of VC capital in foundational LLM labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI capturing 65% of global Q1 2026 VC) creates a gravitational pull that may underfund the causal AI layer precisely when it is most commercially ready. Causal AI startups are competing for attention and capital against companies raising $20–122B in single rounds.
