**Vertical 1 — Marketing Measurement (Most Advanced)**

This places healthcare and financial services in the **chasm-crossing phase**: proof points exist but standardization and scale remain ahead.

**Vertical 3 — General Enterprise Decision Intelligence (Early Adopter)**
The causaLens Digital Worker Factory and decisionOS represent an ambitious generalization thesis — that causal AI agents can replace probabilistic automation across arbitrary enterprise decisions. The 20× reliability benchmark claim and the sub-24-hour deployment promise are the right commercialization vectors, but the enterprise customer list (Cisco, GE Vernova, LG) while credible is not yet a network effect. This segment is still in proof-of-value mode.

Three structural shifts are visible in the data:

1. **From bespoke to productized**: The entire stack — from structure learning (DeepCausalMMM, causal-learn) to inference (DoWhy, EconML) to deployment (causaLens Digital Worker Factory, PyMC AI Agent) — is undergoing productization simultaneously. Fragmented intellectual frameworks are a commercial barrier; convergence enables interoperability and sales.

3. **From explanation to action**: The shift from causal inference (understanding what happened and why) to causal AI (deciding what to do next and deploying that decision autonomously) is the key commercial transition. The Digital Worker Factory and IBM agent partnership signal that at least one major vendor is betting on that transition happening at enterprise scale within the current product cycle.
