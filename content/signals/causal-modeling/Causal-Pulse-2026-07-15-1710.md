## Market Position

Causal AI is past the "explain the concept" phase and into the "embed in a workflow" phase. The signals this week cluster around three adoption patterns: (1) infrastructure-level embedding (causaLens shipping inside Snowflake, eliminating data-movement friction), (2) vertical specialization (DevOps root-cause, drug discovery, MMM) rather than horizontal platform plays, and (3) open-source consolidation around a single stack (PyWhy/DoWhy) now that CausalNex is dead. This looks like an early-majority technology-adoption pattern in enterprise tooling — the "general causal AI platform" narrative is fading in favor of domain-embedded causal features, while the underlying open-source layer is standardizing rather than fragmenting. The one place still stuck in early-adopter/laggard territory is regulated clinical/pharma, where the science is ahead of institutional readiness by a wide margin.

## Predictions

- Expect more "causal-as-a-feature" launches (native apps, warehouse-embedded agents, DevOps AIOps add-ons) rather than new standalone causal AI platforms over the next 6-12 months — the causaLens/Snowflake move is a template others will copy.
