## Market Position

Causal AI is in an **early-majority-adjacent phase for narrow verticals, still early-adopter for general enterprise decisioning**:

- **Marketing/MMM** is the most commercially advanced vertical — causal structure (DAGs, geo-lift, incrementality testing) is becoming a competitive requirement, not a differentiator, among MMM vendors. This is the closest thing to "boring, bought-not-sold" causal AI.
- **Drug discovery/biotech** is where the most sophisticated *intervention design* work is happening (COAST, ACE frameworks) — this is causal inference in its truest sense (understanding mechanism, designing minimal interventions), not causal ML. Regulatory attention (FDA) suggests this vertical could professionalize fastest, but revenue is still thin (Causaly's $93M total funding is modest versus foundation-model peers).
- **Horizontal enterprise decision platforms** (causaLens' decisionOS) are pursuing an LLM+causal hybrid strategy — betting that causal reasoning becomes the "trust layer" for agentic AI. This is the most ambitious but least proven bet: enterprise logos (J&J, Cisco, Scotiabank) exist, but no evidence of step-change revenue or funding acceleration this cycle.
- **Open-source consolidation** (CausalNex EOL, PyWhy dominance) suggests the *infrastructure layer* is stabilizing — a precondition for, not evidence of, broader commercial scaling. When tooling consolidates around one standard, it typically precedes (by 12-24 months) a wave of commercial products built on top.

**What's shifting**: The center of gravity is moving from "causal discovery/estimation as a research technique" toward "causal reasoning embedded in agentic decision systems" (causaLens' explicit thesis) and "causal-constrained intervention design" (COAST, ACE in biotech). Both are steps beyond classic causal inference (ATE/CATE estimation) toward operationalized decision-making — but productization proof points remain sparse.
