2. **Formal unification of cognition across substrates.** The "Cognition spaces" arXiv paper, the "de-anthropomorphizing the mind" framework, and continued basal-cognition reviews are pushing toward a shared vocabulary for comparing bacteria, slime molds, tissues, robots, and LLMs on the same axes (agency, goal-directedness, memory). 3. **Synthetic morphology moving from proof-of-concept to platform.** Xenobots → Anthrobots → Neurobots represents a steady broadening of "what tissue can be repurposed into a body." The addition of neural self-organization (Fotowat et al.) and inter-swarm communication (Parsa et al.) suggests the next phase is less about *making* biobots and more about probing their *collective and cognitive* properties — directly feeding the diverse-intelligence research agenda.

**Emerging cross-pollination**: Neural cellular automata (NCA) remain the single strongest technical bridge between this community and mainstream ML — local-rule, self-repairing computation modeled directly on morphogenesis is now an active ML architecture family, not just a metaphor. Meanwhile, game-theoretic/organizational framings (distributed chess) hint at Levin's ideas migrating into organizational design and multi-agent systems thinking outside biology entirely.
