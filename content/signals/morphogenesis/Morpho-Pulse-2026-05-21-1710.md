## Field Trajectory

### The Mechanistic Gap Is Closing

For several years, the most credible scientific objection to Levin's program was a mechanistic one: the pathway from membrane voltage patterns to durable organismal outcomes was asserted more than demonstrated. The Cervera-Levin-Mafe (2026) paper on top-down transcriptional regulation, combined with the Fotowat et al. (2026) work on self-organizing neural networks, represents a concerted effort to fill that gap with formal models and experimental systems. The field is maturing from conceptual provocation to mechanistic demonstration.

### The Regulatory Threshold Has Been Crossed

The Geneva Protocol is a phase transition marker. International regulatory bodies do not convene frameworks for technologies that remain hypothetical or contained to academic settings. The Protocol's naming of Xenobots, Anthrobots, and Neurobots as distinct regulatory categories signals that synthetic morphological organisms are now regarded as a real-world concern — and that the field has moved faster than most observers anticipated. This will reshape the research environment: biosafety-by-design will become a funding requirement, and some experiments currently possible in academic labs may shift to regulated facilities.

### Cross-Pollination With AI Is Accelerating, But Asymmetrically

The traffic between morphogenesis and AI currently flows primarily in one direction: AI researchers are borrowing ideas from bioelectric computation (scale-free agency, multiscale competency, goal-directedness without central control) to address problems in multi-agent systems, reward specification, and embodied cognition. The reverse flow — morphogenesis research directly using AI tools — is present but thinner, mostly confined to ML-guided organoid monitoring and computational bioelectric modeling (BETSE, GABEE). Expect this asymmetry to persist for 2–3 years, then potentially invert as AI-guided perturbation of bioelectric systems becomes experimentally tractable.

### The Theoretical Architecture Is Being Formalized Deliberately

The "Mind Everywhere: Part One" structure, the "Cognition All the Way Down 2.0" framing, and the chess-as-collective-intelligence paper are not independent outputs — they represent a coordinated effort to build a formal, philosophically defensible framework for diverse intelligence that can withstand scrutiny from multiple disciplines simultaneously. Levin is publishing across biology, philosophy of mind, and cognitive science venues in what looks like a deliberate flanking strategy: establish the framework in each disciplinary vernacular before critics in any one field can frame the terms of debate. This is unusual scientific strategy and worth tracking as a model for how heterodox scientific programs seek legitimacy.

### Organoid Intelligence Is the Near-Term Convergence Point

The intersection of brain organoids, bioelectric computation, and synthetic morphology is where the most significant near-term developments will likely occur. The Varley et al. finding of brain-like information architectures in *pre-neural* embryonic tissue, combined with the organoid intelligence program and ML-guided organoid monitoring (Trends in Biotechnology, 2026), creates a research stack that could, within 3–5 years, produce biological computing systems with no clear precedent in either computer science or neuroscience.
