## Field Trajectory

**The field is undergoing a quiet but structurally significant shift: from demonstration to formalization.**

The first decade of modern bioelectricity research (roughly 2012–2022) was dominated by striking demonstrations — two-headed planaria, Xenobots, memory in organisms without brains. The current phase is characterized by three convergent moves:

**1. Toward unified theory.** The "Cognition Spaces" preprint and the TAME framework both reflect pressure to stop treating each bioelectric phenomenon as an isolated curiosity and start building comparative, predictive theory. The field is borrowing the toolkit of complexity science — dimensionality, phase spaces, emergence — to do this. Whether these frameworks will produce testable quantitative predictions, rather than taxonomies, remains the open question.

**2. Toward human-relevant substrates.** The Anthrobot lifecycle paper is the clearest marker here. Moving from amphibian to human cells is not merely a translational step — it changes the ethical and regulatory environment entirely. The inclusion of bioethicists (Lunshof) as co-authors on organoid papers suggests the research community is anticipating this shift and trying to get ahead of it institutionally.

**3. Toward AI hybridization.** The machine learning / organoid integration paper in *Trends in Biotechnology*, the "Cognition Spaces" framework positioning AI and biological systems on the same axes, and Levin's AGI conference presence all point toward a convergence thesis: that biological and artificial intelligence are not categorically distinct but occupy different regions of a shared possibility space. This is becoming a research program, not just a philosophical claim.

**The emerging cross-pollination story** is that morphogenesis is offering AI research something it cannot generate internally: existence proofs for intelligence without explicit programming, without gradient descent, and without centralized architecture. For AI researchers worried about brittleness, alignment, and the limits of current paradigms, bioelectric computation is increasingly legible as a research direction rather than a curiosity.

**What is notably absent** from this week's findings: direct empirical engagement from the AI/ML mainstream with bioelectric computation as a design inspiration (as opposed to a philosophical reference). That gap is the most important thing to watch close.
