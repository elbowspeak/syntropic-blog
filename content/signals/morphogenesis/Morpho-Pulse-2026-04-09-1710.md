## Field Trajectory

### The Convergence Thesis Is Becoming Empirical

For most of the past decade, the intellectual core of this field — that cognition, morphogenesis, and agency are expressions of the same underlying computational logic operating at different scales — has been primarily theoretical. The 2026 data suggests this is rapidly changing. The Varley et al. paper finding brain-like information architectures in pre-neural tissue, the ACS Synthetic Biology paper engineering learning primitives from scratch, and the Fotowat et al. paper linking neural self-organization to gene expression and behavior collectively represent empirical tests of what were previously conceptual claims. The field is in the early stages of an experimental confirmation phase.

### Bioelectric Medicine Has a Clear Clinical Entry Point

The cancer oncology trajectory is distinct from the regeneration trajectory in maturity. Ion channel modulators for breast cancer are in combination therapy trials; limb regeneration is at frog proof-of-concept with rodent models in progress. This asymmetry is important: oncology will likely produce the first clinical bioelectric medicine results, establishing regulatory precedent and de-risking the mechanism for subsequent regenerative applications. The field should expect oncology data — positive or negative — to significantly reshape investment and regulatory attitudes toward bioelectric medicine broadly within the next 3–5 years.

### The Anthropomorphism Inversion

A subtle but important shift is visible in how Levin's recent talks and papers frame the relationship between biological and artificial intelligence. Earlier framing emphasized that biology is "already doing" what AI aspires to do (collective intelligence, robust goal-directedness, fault tolerance). The 2026 framing — particularly in the "Mind Everywhere" papers and the AI/consciousness podcast appearances — increasingly runs the argument in the opposite direction: AI systems may help us understand and communicate with biological intelligences we cannot yet interpret. The Fauna Systems mission statement ("communicating novel goals to biological materials") and the "Detecting and Communicating with Unconventional Beings" talk title (May 2025, ici Berlin) both reflect this inversion. The field is moving from "biology as inspiration for AI" toward "AI as interpreter of biology."

### Cross-Pollination Vectors Are Multiplying

Three distinct cross-pollination pathways are now active simultaneously:
1. **Biology → AI architecture**: HADES evolutionary strategy; organicist principles informing ML design
2. **AI → biology**: Machine learning solving the bioelectric inverse problem; AI-guided organoid synthesis; robotic automation of cell culture
3. **Biology + AI → synthetic living systems**: Fauna Systems' AI-designed xenobots; Anthrobot therapeutic design pipelines

This tripartite cross-pollination is qualitatively different from the earlier unidirectional "bio-inspired computing" paradigm. It creates feedback loops that could accelerate both fields simultaneously in ways that are difficult to predict from within either discipline.
