## Field Trajectory

### The Central Convergence: Biology as Computation, Computation as Biology

The field is undergoing a slow but accelerating conceptual unification that, if it holds, will be recognized as one of the more significant intellectual developments of the 2020s. Three previously separate research traditions are merging:

1. **Developmental biology and morphogenesis** — historically focused on genetic programs and chemical gradients
2. **Cognitive science and AI** — historically focused on neural computation and symbolic/statistical reasoning
3. **Complexity science and information theory** — historically focused on emergence, self-organization, and causal structure

The Allen Discovery Center's simultaneous publication on LLM hypothesis generation, causal emergence, brain-like architectures in embryonic tissue, and bioelectric field-mediated prepatterning in a single year signals that this is not accidental cross-pollination — it reflects a deliberate theoretical agenda to establish morphogenesis as a form of *computation*, and computation as a form of *cognition*, in a framework that applies across all scales of organized matter.

### Where the Connections Are Sharpening

**Morphogenesis → AI Architecture**: The cognitive light cone concept and multi-scale competency hierarchy are beginning to influence thinking about AI system design. The question "how does biological agency scale from cell to organism?" has a direct AI analog in "how should goal-directed behavior scale from module to system?" Expect to see these ideas appear in robotics and multi-agent AI papers with increasing frequency over the next 18–24 months.

**Bioelectrics → Drug Discovery**: The identification of ~150 approved ion channel drugs as potential repurposing candidates for bioelectric intervention is a near-term commercial opportunity that doesn't require waiting for the longer arc of synthetic biology or regenerative medicine to mature. This is the field's most actionable near-term connection to existing pharmaceutical infrastructure.

**Basal Cognition → Consciousness Science**: The identification of brain-like information architectures in pre-neural embryonic tissue, combined with the planarian non-neural memory findings, is generating serious theoretical pressure on neural-centric theories of consciousness. This is creating unexpected cross-traffic between the Levin lab's empirical program and philosophy of mind, active inference (Karl Friston's free energy principle), and integrated information theory.

**Organoid Intelligence → Biocomputing**: The convergence of 3D MEA electrophysiology, microfluidic perfusion, and human iPSC-derived organoids is creating a new class of hybrid biological-digital computing substrate. The reported ability of cortical spheroids to learn simple tasks and organoid chips to recognize speech (2023–2024) suggests this is moving faster than most observers anticipated. Energy efficiency relative to silicon computing is the most commercially interesting near-term metric to watch.

### The Underappreciated Thread: Memory Without Neurons

Across planarians, slime molds, bacterial biofilms, and embryonic organoids, a consistent theme is emerging: *memory* — the ability to store and retrieve information about past states in ways that influence future behavior — is not a neural phenomenon. It is a property of organized biological matter operating through ion channels, gap junctions, transcriptional networks, and external environmental traces. This is not a fringe claim anymore; it is accumulating independent empirical support from multiple model systems and multiple research groups. The implications for how we define cognition, design therapeutic interventions, and build synthetic biological systems are substantial and largely unexplored.
