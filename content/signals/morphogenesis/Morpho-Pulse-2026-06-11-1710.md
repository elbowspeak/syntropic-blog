## Field Trajectory

**The field is undergoing a transition from descriptive to predictive and engineering-facing.**

For most of its modern history (2010–2022), bioelectric morphogenesis research was primarily demonstrating *that* bioelectric signals matter — showing correlations between voltage states and anatomical outcomes, disrupting gap junctions and observing pattern changes, cataloguing ion channel expression in developing embryos. The planarian work with cryptic phenotypes and the noise-resistant genetic switch paper both signal a shift: the field is now building *models that predict outcomes before perturbation* and *design principles that specify desired computational properties*. This is the transition from phenomenology to engineering.

**Three convergence lines are now clearly visible:**

1. **Bioelectricity × AI**: The relationship is becoming bidirectional and productive. AI (evolutionary algorithms, ML) is being used to design xenobot morphologies; simultaneously, bioelectric networks are being proposed as architectural inspiration for AI systems that need to be robust, distributed, and goal-directed without central control. The Neurobots paper is the sharpest current expression of this convergence — a living system with self-organizing neural architecture that was itself AI-designed.

2. **Morphogenesis × Complexity Science**: The search-efficiency formalization in "Cognition all the way down 2.0" is an explicit bridge to complexity science and information theory. Framing biological problem-solving as search in multi-scale problem spaces connects directly to concepts from algorithmic information theory, fitness landscape theory, and statistical physics. This opens the field to quantitative tools from those communities that have not previously been applied to morphogenesis.

3. **Basal Cognition × Ethics/Governance**: The Geneva Protocol signal (even at low confidence) and the "third state" framing in the ECOticias piece reflect a broader cultural processing of what the field has been producing. The entities being created — Neurobots, Anthrobots, Scrub-Bots — are increasingly difficult to classify, and governance is beginning to respond. The field will increasingly need to develop its own ethical vocabulary and engage proactively with regulatory bodies rather than waiting for frameworks to be imposed.

**The cross-pollination story is underappreciated in mainstream science media.** Levin's "cognitive glue" framework is quietly influencing how people in organizational theory and distributed AI systems think about coherence and agency — the idea that what makes a collective a *self* is the information-processing layer that integrates goals across components, not the components themselves. This conceptual export is happening faster in podcasts, essays, and invited talks than in formal citation networks.
