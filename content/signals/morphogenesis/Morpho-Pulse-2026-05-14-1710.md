## Field Trajectory

The field is undergoing a **phase transition from descriptive to predictive and prescriptive science**. The first decade of this program was largely establishing that bioelectricity *matters* — that ion channels and gap junctions encode pattern information, that membrane voltage pre-patterns can be read and rewritten, that non-neural cells exhibit cognitive-like behaviors. That case is now substantially made.

The current trajectory has three converging vectors:

**1. Formalization and quantification.** The Anthrobot lifecycle paper, the TAME/RL paper, and the membrane potential/transcription work in *Scientific Reports* all represent a shift toward rigorous, quantitative modeling. The field is building the mathematical substrate needed to make falsifiable, predictive claims about morphogenetic computation — not just suggestive demonstrations.

**2. Cross-pollination with AI and complexity science is accelerating.** The chess-as-collective-intelligence paper and the two-armed-bandit slime mold work are not peripheral curiosities — they represent deliberate attempts to establish formal equivalences between biological cognition and computational frameworks. The TAME/RL connection is particularly significant: if reinforcement learning can be formalized as a description of what cells do during development, it creates a two-way bridge where AI researchers gain biological intuitions and biologists gain powerful new modeling tools. This could reshape how both fields think about agency, reward, and goal-directedness.

**3. The consciousness question is moving to center stage.** The density of Levin's April 2025 appearances on consciousness, panpsychism, and pre-biological minds suggests the field is approaching the point where it must formally engage with the "hard problem" — not as philosophy but as empirical science. The collaboration with Terrence Deacon is a notable signal here, as Deacon's framework provides a naturalistic account of intentionality and absence-based causation that could give Levin's cognitive-layer thesis more rigorous philosophical grounding.

**Emerging connection to watch**: The gap between "bioelectric networks process morphogenetic information" and "bioelectric networks are a form of cognition" is narrowing rapidly. The field is moving toward a unified theory in which pattern memory, goal-directedness, and scaling of mind are all aspects of the same underlying computational architecture — one that predates nervous systems by hundreds of millions of years and may offer genuinely new design principles for AI systems.
