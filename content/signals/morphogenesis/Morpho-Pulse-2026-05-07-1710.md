## Field Trajectory

The field is undergoing a transition from *description* to *engineering* — and from *biological* to *computational* framing.

**The descriptive phase** (roughly 2012–2022) established that bioelectric signals are real, functional, and causally upstream of morphogenetic outcomes. The planarian experiments, the left-right asymmetry work, and the Xenobot demonstrations built the empirical foundation. That foundation is now sufficiently credible that the field no longer needs to spend most of its energy on proof-of-concept demonstrations.

**The engineering phase** now emerging has three distinct vectors:

1. **Intervention design**: Computational modeling (as in the IEEE planarian paper) is beginning to make it possible to *infer* what bioelectric interventions would repair a given phenotype, rather than relying on trial-and-error experimentation. The morphoceutical framework — targeting anatomical homeostasis setpoints — is the clinical expression of this.

2. **Synthetic construction**: Xenobots, Anthrobots, and now memory-competent biobots demonstrate that morphogenetic and cognitive competencies can be instantiated in novel living substrates. The GECCO prompt-to-intervention paper and the ACS Synthetic Biology minimal-circuits paper show this engineering is becoming programmable and language-like.

3. **Theoretical unification**: The basal cognition program is building toward a substrate-independent theory of agency and learning — one in which neurons are a late evolutionary specialization of more ancient cellular information-processing machinery. The reinforcement learning–to–agency frameworks, the slime mold habituation modeling, and the propofol results all converge on this.

**The most important emerging connection** is between morphogenesis and AI — not just AI as a tool for analyzing morphogenetic data, but AI concepts (prompts, interventions, goals, agency) being mapped onto biological systems as interpretive frameworks, and vice versa. The Levin-Bongard collaboration is the clearest institutional locus of this cross-pollination. There is a plausible medium-term future in which "morphogenetic programming" becomes a genuine discipline, drawing equally from developmental biology, evolutionary computation, and LLM-style interface design.

**Complexity science connections** are also deepening. The framing of bodies as "goal-directed collectives" of semi-autonomous agents — each cell pursuing local objectives within a larger coherent system — maps directly onto multi-agent systems research and swarm intelligence. The planetary intelligence framing emerging in symposium contexts (the ICI Berlin "Scales of Life" event) suggests the field is beginning to theorize *upward* as well: from cells to organisms to ecosystems, looking for common computational principles across scales.
