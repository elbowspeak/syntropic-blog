TSMC's CoWoS bottleneck and the Taiwan-only packaging dependency mean that even successful Arizona fab ramp-ups do not proportionally increase deliverable AI accelerator supply. *The true constraint has shifted downstream of the fab.*

**Export controls → open-weight frontier model development (weak causal link):** GLM-5's training on Huawei Ascend at frontier performance levels updates the causal model. U.S. export controls on Nvidia GPUs appear insufficient to prevent frontier-class open-weight model development in China. The mechanism being disrupted is *speed and efficiency of training*, not *ability to reach frontier capability*.

**Inference cost deflation → application layer value migration:** As inference costs approach commodity pricing, the locus of value in the AI supply chain continues migrating upward — from chips → training → inference → toward data, proprietary workflows, and application-layer differentiation. DeepSeek's $0.28/M token pricing accelerates this shift.

**Model weight leakage → new distribution control architecture:** The Claude Mythos leak-then-contain sequence establishes a precedent: advanced dual-use models will increasingly be distributed through credentialed consortium structures rather than open release or standard API access. This creates a new category of supply chain gatekeeping at the model layer.
