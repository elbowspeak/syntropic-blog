## Investment Implications

### Becoming More Attractive

**SK Hynix** — M15X launch and HBM4 mass production timeline, combined with >50% market share target, position it as the structurally dominant HBM supplier heading into the Rubin cycle. The $14.6B investment appears well-timed against a supply-constrained demand curve. Near-term risk: execution on yields at HBM4.

**Broadcom** — The Meta custom silicon co-development deal adds another hyperscaler anchor to a custom ASIC business already serving Google. Broadcom is emerging as the default custom silicon design partner for hyperscalers that want NVIDIA independence, a structurally high-margin, high-moat position. AVGO deserves a premium in any AI infrastructure portfolio.

**TSMC** — Doubling its 2nm expansion pace while simultaneously being the sole credible manufacturer for the most advanced AI chips (Rubin, future Apple, Broadcom ASICs) means TSMC is the irreplaceable chokepoint of the entire AI supply chain. The 5-fab 2nm target by year-end signals confidence in sustained demand.

**CoreWeave** — As the launch partner for NVIDIA Rubin in H2 2026, CoreWeave gains a meaningful first-mover window in next-gen GPU cloud capacity. However, the ~80% H100 price collapse is a reminder that GPU cloud economics are brutally cyclical — watch for whether Rubin-era differentiation (Spectrum-X efficiency) translates to better margin durability than the H100 cohort.

**NVIDIA** — $5.26T market cap reflects current dominance, but the causal signals here (hyperscaler custom silicon acceleration, Chinese open-weights reducing inference revenue pools, AMD-Intel x86 AI unification) all represent 2nd-degree pressure on the long-term TAM. NVIDIA remains the safest near-term infrastructure bet (Rubin cycle, enterprise adoption wave), but the moat is being systematically attacked from multiple directions. The risk/reward at $5.26T is less compelling than 18 months ago.

### Becoming Less Attractive / Watch Risks

**Incumbent GPU Cloud Providers (generic H100 capacity players)** — The ~80% spot price collapse is not a temporary dislocation; it reflects structural oversupply of H100-class hardware. Providers without differentiated software stacks, Rubin-gen upgrades, or enterprise contracts are facing sustained margin compression. Avoid undifferentiated GPU rental businesses.

**Intel** — The Google-Intel Xeon collaboration and Terafab-18A wins are genuinely positive signals, but they exist against the backdrop of Broadcom-Meta and continued hyperscaler custom silicon momentum eroding Intel's traditional data centre CPU business. The 18A node remains unproven at volume. Intel is a high-variance trade: execution on foundry could be transformational; another yield stumble could be existential.

**Western Proprietary Inference API Businesses** — The Chinese open-weights cost floor (<1/3 of Claude Opus 4.7 pricing) is a direct threat to the revenue models of companies whose value proposition is inference access rather than proprietary models or enterprise integration. Pure inference API players without strong enterprise lock-in or regulatory moats face structural pricing pressure.

**OpenAI (Pre-IPO Valuation Risk)** — The $852B post-money valuation against ~$20B annualized revenue implies a ~42x revenue multiple. The $280B 2030 revenue projection would require extraordinary execution in an environment where inference is commoditizing, Chinese open-weights are closing the capability gap, and hyperscalers are building custom silicon. The IPO signal is worth watching — retail tranche inclusion suggests a desire to broaden the holder base before competitive pressures become more visible in financial results.

### Thematic Calls

**Power & Cooling Infrastructure** — TSMC's 5-fab 2nm expansion, Rubin's data centre deployments, and the Stargate build all create compounding demand for power infrastructure, advanced cooling, and grid interconnection. Vertiv, Eaton, Schneider Electric, and specialized cooling vendors remain structurally well-positioned regardless of which chip or model wins.

**Radiation-Hardened / Space-Grade Components** — Terafab's orbital AI data centre ambition (1M satellites, D3 processor) is speculative in timeline but directionally creates a new supply chain vertical. If it progresses, it will be severely supply-constrained given the near-absence of rad-hard semiconductor capacity at scale. Early positioning in this niche (e.g., BAE Systems, Microchip Technology's space division) could be asymmetric.

**Agentic Workflow Software** — ClawBench's 33.3% best score on real-world agentic tasks signals that the capability gap between current models and reliable agentic deployment remains large. Software companies building human-in-the-loop orchestration, error recovery, and workflow reliability layers are addressing a real and durable gap — this is where enterprise value will accrete as raw inference costs commoditize.
