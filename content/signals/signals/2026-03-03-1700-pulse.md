## Investment Implications

### Becoming More Attractive

**Energy Infrastructure & Power Generation**
- The most structurally significant signal in today's data is the explicit confirmation that *power*, not chips, is now the binding AI constraint. - **Theme:** Behind-the-meter power contracts, grid interconnection capacity, and firm-dispatchable generation assets near existing data center corridors.

**HBM/Advanced Memory Manufacturers**
- 70–80% DRAM/HBM price increases represent a significant margin opportunity for SK Hynix, Samsung Semiconductor, and Micron. HBM supply is structurally constrained. This is a high-conviction near-term earnings tailwind.
- **Risk:** Capacity additions in late 2026–2027 could create an oversupply correction; cyclical dynamics remain.

**Private Cloud / On-Premise AI Infrastructure**
- Sovereign AI mandates + CIO cost pressure + geopolitical data fragmentation are converging to make private cloud and on-premise deployment more attractive. Vendors enabling private AI infrastructure (networking, storage, accelerator integration) benefit structurally from this fragmentation trend.

**Specialized AI Cloud Providers (Cost-Competitive Tier)**
- The 37%+ cost gap versus hyperscalers is now material enough to drive real workload migration for inference-heavy, cost-sensitive applications. Providers with owned or directly-leased GPU capacity (not resellers) are better positioned.

**Critical Minerals Producers (Long-Duration)**
