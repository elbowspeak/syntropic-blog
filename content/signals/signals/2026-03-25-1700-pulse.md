## Causal Updates

**1. Inference volume growth is *overwhelming* per-token cost deflation.**
The 280-fold drop in per-token costs is being more than offset by a 320% rise in total inference spending. This breaks the naive assumption that "cheaper inference = lower GPU demand." The correct model is: cheaper inference → broader deployment → more total tokens → more hardware needed. This is Jevons Paradox operating in real time in AI infrastructure.

**2. Custom silicon is now a credible threat to NVIDIA's hyperscaler revenue — but only at inference.**
Meta's MTIA roadmap, Midjourney's TPU migration, and Google's TPU sell-outs collectively signal that the training market remains NVIDIA-dominated (GB200-NVL72 underpins GPT-5.2), but inference is bifurcating. Cost-sensitive, high-volume, fixed-workload inference (image generation, recommendation) is migrating. Dynamic, frontier-model inference is not — yet.

**3. TSMC's pre-sold Fab 4 and AWS Capacity Block price hikes are the same signal from two different vantage points.**
Supply of leading-edge AI compute capacity is genuinely scarce at the infrastructure level. TSMC is sold out before building. AWS is rationing via price. These are not independent events — they reflect a single underlying constraint: CoWoS/SoIC advanced packaging and HBM are the real bottlenecks, not fab wafers alone.

**4. CHIPS Act uncertainty is creating a timing mismatch risk.**
Companies that pre-positioned U.S. manufacturing capacity (Intel Fab 52, TSMC Arizona) made multi-year bets partly on federal subsidy predictability. Funding cancellations and political uncertainty don't reverse the physical investments — but they pressure unit economics and could delay second-phase expansions.
