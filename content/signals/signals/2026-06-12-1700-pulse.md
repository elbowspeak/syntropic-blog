## Causal Updates

**New cause-effect relationships observed:**

1. **Jevons Paradox is now confirmed operative in AI inference.** Unit cost deflation (-280x) is not reducing total compute demand — it is expanding it. This means each successive round of efficiency improvements (better models, cheaper chips, optimized inference) will likely *increase* rather than decrease total GPU demand. Supply chain planners should not model cost reduction as a demand dampener.

2. **Chinese open-weight model pricing (Qwen 3.7 Max, DeepSeek lineage) is structurally compressing Western API margins.** This is not a one-time event but an ongoing competitive dynamic that forces US frontier labs to respond with pricing or efficiency, feeding back into inference volume growth and GPU demand.

3. **Inference cost dominance (85% of AI budgets) is now redirecting semiconductor R&D priorities.** The industry is visibly pivoting toward inference-optimized silicon (custom accelerators, Inferentia, Trainium) and advanced packaging (HBM4E, hybrid bonding) as the primary value creation vectors — training silicon is becoming relatively commoditized.

4. **Power infrastructure has become a co-equal supply chain constraint alongside silicon.** The Ratepayer Protection Pledge and 25% electricity demand projection signal that energy availability, not chip availability, may become the binding constraint on data center buildout within the 2027–2030 window.

5. **Advanced packaging is now the primary semiconductor scaling vector.** With classical node shrinks slowing, the Imec/EV Group hybrid bonding milestone and Samsung HBM4E shipment confirm that interconnect density and 3D stacking are where performance gains are being manufactured — shifting supply chain criticality toward packaging equipment and substrate suppliers.

**Inference Optimization Software & Silicon** — As inference becomes 85% of AI budgets, tools that reduce inference cost become high-ROI enterprise purchases. **Cerebras**, **Groq**, **Fireworks AI**, and AWS's **Inferentia/Trainium** ecosystem benefit. In public markets, watch **ARM Holdings** whose architecture underlies many inference-optimized designs.

**Samsung Electronics** — Discounted relative to SK Hynix on HBM exposure, but HBM4E sample shipments suggest Samsung is closing the qualification gap. A valuation catch-up trade if Nvidia qualifies Samsung HBM4E for production volumes.
