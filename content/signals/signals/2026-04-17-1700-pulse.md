**Nuclear power operators and uranium producers** — Amazon, Microsoft, and now broader hyperscaler demand is creating direct offtake for nuclear power. Palisades restarts, Three Mile Island, and Susquehanna deals validate the thesis. Cameco (CCO), Constellation Energy (CEG), and NuScale remain relevant. Market may still be underpricing the structural, decade-long nature of this demand.

**Google (Alphabet) — TPU infrastructure play** — Midjourney's $16.8M annualized savings on TPU v6e is a live customer testimonial. Google benefits both as the TPU vendor and as the cloud host. As more inference customers run the same cost analysis, Google Cloud's TPU offering becomes a genuine differentiator against AWS and Azure.

**Power infrastructure & grid equipment** — Transmission, switchgear, transformer manufacturers (e.g., Eaton, Hubbell, Vertiv) face sustained demand as data center buildouts run into grid interconnection bottlenecks. Power scarcity as the binding constraint means these companies are now critical path for AI infrastructure.

**Huawei Ascend supply chain (non-investable directly for most Western investors, but relevant for risk)** — GLM-5's success validates Ascend at scale. Chinese semiconductor equipment, memory, and packaging suppliers feeding the Ascend ecosystem are gaining strategic importance.

### Becoming Less Attractive / Risks to Watch

**NVIDIA inference revenue** — Midjourney's migration is the canary. At $1.49–$1.89/hour for H100s on spot markets, the economics increasingly favor TPUs or custom ASICs for inference. NVIDIA's training moat remains intact (Rubin demand is real), but inference is a growing revenue segment at risk. Market may be pricing NVIDIA as if inference revenue is as defensible as training revenue — this is increasingly questionable.

**Pure-play inference API / LLM wrapper businesses** — At $0.04/M tokens for capable open-weight models, the margin available to intermediaries is approaching zero. Companies whose business model is "we call GPT-4 and mark it up" face existential pressure. Not publicly listed as a category, but relevant for private market and VC exposure.

**UK AI and data center ecosystem** — Stargate pause is a concrete signal that the UK is losing the infrastructure investment race due to power pricing and regulatory uncertainty. Companies dependent on UK data center buildout (UK-focused colocation, construction, power contractors) face a slower ramp than peers in US/Middle East.

**US chip companies with China revenue exposure** — The WEF finding that China rejected newly permitted US hardware confirms the revenue loss is structural, not cyclical. Any investment thesis that depended on US export control relaxation restoring China revenue for companies like Intel, Qualcomm, or others should be revisited.

### Key Valuation Disconnect to Watch
The market is pricing AI infrastructure as if power is a solvable near-term problem. The combination of Stargate UK pause, Ireland reaching 21% national electricity demand from data centers, and Greenpeace projecting 11x demand growth by 2030 suggests power is a **multi-year structural bottleneck** — not a 12-month procurement problem. Companies that own or control power assets (nuclear operators, dedicated renewable offtake holders) may be significantly undervalued relative to compute hardware companies whose expansion is ultimately gated by power availability.
