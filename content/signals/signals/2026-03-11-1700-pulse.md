With hyperscaler capex at $600B+ pulling AI chip demand, TSMC's fab utilization at N3/N2 looks structurally full. *Risk: geopolitical Taiwan exposure, U.S.-Iran spillover affecting shipping lanes.*

**SK Hynix / Samsung (Memory division)**
Planned 20% HBM3E price hikes into a demand environment where hyperscalers have pre-committed $600B gives these suppliers rare pricing power. SK Hynix in particular, as the leading HBM supplier to NVIDIA, is in a structurally advantaged position. *Risk: if AI capex cycle turns, HBM oversupply could follow.*

**U.S. Data Center Power & Cooling Infrastructure**
$600B capex concentrated ~75% in AI infrastructure means massive demand for power delivery, cooling systems, and grid interconnection. Energy cost uncertainty from oil prices adds urgency to efficiency investments. Companies in this supply chain (power electronics, liquid cooling) face a multi-year demand runway.

**Texas Instruments**
The Sherman fab coming online represents a long-term structural cost advantage — domestic 300mm analog production at scale. TI's patient capex strategy (started 2022, producing 2026) is now beginning to pay forward. Analog demand tied to automotive and industrial AI edge deployment is a secondary tailwind.
