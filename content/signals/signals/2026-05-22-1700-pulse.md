## Causal Updates

**1. Custom Silicon → HBM Tightness → Price Hikes → OEM Margin Compression**
The acceleration of custom ASIC deployments (Google TPU 8, AWS Trainium, Meta MTIA) alongside H200/Rubin demand is creating a *combined* HBM demand surge that neither Samsung nor SK Hynix anticipated at this pace. The 20% planned price hike is a lagging indicator — the supply constraint likely began in Q1 2026. This creates a cost pass-through chain: memory makers → GPU/ASIC integrators → OEM server builders → cloud providers → enterprise buyers, with each layer absorbing or forwarding margin pressure.

**2. Inference Cost Collapse → Infrastructure Demand Paradox**
Per-token costs falling ~1,000x is not reducing infrastructure spend — it is *increasing* it via induced demand (Jevons paradox at scale). Cheaper inference enables more use cases, more tokens consumed, more compute required. This explains why $600B in hyperscaler capex coexists with sub-$1/M token pricing. The supply chain implication: volume, not margin, is the growth driver for infrastructure vendors.

**3. Geopolitical Fragmentation → Sovereign AI Buildouts → Redundant Supply Chain Demand**
Nations pursuing AI sovereignty are creating parallel, partially redundant demand for compute, data center construction, and energy infrastructure outside the primary US/China supply chains. This structurally increases total addressable demand for AI infrastructure components — especially in Gulf states (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar) making strategic infrastructure investments — but also fragments standards and creates compliance complexity for multinational suppliers.

**4. TPU Scaling → Reduced NVIDIA Training Revenue Exposure**
Google's ability to train across 1M+ TPUs across distributed sites means the largest single AI training customer is systematically de-risking its NVIDIA dependency for pretraining workloads. This is a slow-moving but structurally important cause-effect: each TPU generation narrows the performance gap with H100/H200 for Google's specific workloads, reducing the TAM for merchant GPU training at hyperscaler scale.
