## Investment Implications

### Becoming More Attractive

**TSMC** — RTX Spark (3N) + Rubin production simultaneously increases 3N and advanced packaging utilization. Two major NVIDIA product cycles running in parallel is a revenue concentration *and* volume upside story. Near-term allocation tightness likely supports pricing power.

**MediaTek** — The 18% Arm Holdings surge and 5% MediaTek gain reflect the market correctly identifying that co-designing a chip with NVIDIA is a franchise-level event. MediaTek now has credibility in premium/AI silicon that its prior mobile-centric positioning did not afford. Re-rating may have further to run if Spark design wins are confirmed.

**Arm Holdings** — The PC architecture is shifting. Arm's royalty model means every RTX Spark unit shipped generates revenue. The 18% single-day move is large, but the structural thesis (x86 displacement in AI PCs, data center Arm adoption) is multi-year and arguably still early.

**Nuclear power operators & SMR developers** — Amazon's $650M nuclear campus acquisition and Microsoft's Three Mile Island restart are not one-off deals; they signal a repeatable procurement template. Operators of existing nuclear plants with adjacent land and grid interconnect are underappreciated infrastructure assets in the AI supply chain.

**Google (Alphabet)** — The Midjourney TPU migration is a meaningful commercial validation of TPU v6e as a cost-competitive inference platform. If this becomes a repeatable case study, Google Cloud's AI infrastructure business has a differentiated margin story that the market may be underweighting relative to AWS/Azure.

**Power infrastructure & grid interconnect companies** — The 50% QoQ drop in US data center starts is a demand signal for anyone who can solve the power bottleneck: transmission equipment manufacturers, grid software, backup power, and behind-the-meter generation.

**Intel** — The 4.5% single-day move is the market sending a clear message. RTX Spark directly targets the segment where Intel has historically been most defensible (the PC). **Hyperscalers' free cash flow (particularly Amazon)** — $600B+ in collective capex with Amazon projected at negative FCF of $17–28B this year is a stress signal. If AI revenue monetization does not accelerate materially in H2 2026, expect capex guidance reductions in Q3/Q4 earnings — which would be a significant negative for the entire AI hardware supply chain.
