The combination of confirmed demand (eight cloud partners deploying Rubin H2 2026) and state-backed supply expansion makes SK Hynix the clearest memory supply chain play.

**AI FinOps / Cost Attribution Software (private market)**
The finding that organizations' reported AI spend is ~12x lower than actual AI-driven cloud consumption is a large, underappreciated market signal. The 97.5% "ghost spend" problem is not a rounding error — it's a structural gap that will force enterprise procurement and finance teams to adopt dedicated attribution tooling. Early-stage companies solving this problem are likely undervalued relative to the scale of the problem.

**Power / Grid Infrastructure**
2,500+ GW stuck in interconnection queues means entities with secured grid rights are holding a scarce, non-replicable asset. This includes: independent power producers with data center PPAs already signed, nuclear operators (zero-carbon baseload with no queue), and transmission infrastructure operators. The South Korea plan implicitly requires massive grid investment alongside fab construction — Korean utility and grid equipment exposure may be underpriced.
