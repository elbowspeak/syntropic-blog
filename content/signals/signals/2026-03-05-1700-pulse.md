The 2028 Tainan fab adds long-duration growth optionality. *Risk: geopolitical Taiwan exposure remains unpriced by many Western portfolios.*

**Nvidia (NVDA)** — Blackwell's 4–10x cost-per-token improvement is a classic platform lock-in event: it makes Nvidia the default inference substrate while simultaneously expanding the total market through demand elasticity. The H200 ramp adds near-term revenue visibility. *Risk: Midjourney's TPU migration is a leading indicator of workload-specific GPU substitution that could accelerate.*

**Google (GOOGL) / Google Cloud** — TPU v6e's validated 67% inference cost reduction over Nvidia A100/H100 is a significant competitive differentiator. Gemini 3.1 Pro's 1M context window and Vertex AI billing expansion indicate Google is successfully monetizing its vertically integrated TPU/model stack. *Risk: enterprise sales cycle for cloud switching remains long.*

**Energy Infrastructure & Grid Technology** — Data center power demand nearly tripling by 2028 against constrained permitting creates durable pricing power for: independent power producers in low-permitting-friction geographies, demand-response and grid software companies, and modular nuclear developers. This is a multi-year structural tailwind not dependent on any single AI product cycle.

**Marvell (MRVL)** — The XConn acquisition positions Marvell directly in CXL/PCIe switching, a critical bottleneck as AI clusters scale beyond single-GPU compute. This is a strategic capability gap-fill at a time when interconnect is becoming a primary performance constraint.

**API-only AI inference businesses** — The simultaneous release of high-quality open-weights models (GPT-oss-120b, DeepSeek-V3.2, GLM-5 MIT license) and 4–10x Blackwell cost reductions compresses the moat for pure-play inference API providers.
