## Causal Updates

**2nm validation → wafer allocation tightening:** AMD Venice entering N2 production confirms TSMC's 2nm node is yield-viable for non-mobile workloads. With NVIDIA Rubin, Apple, and now AMD all competing for N2 capacity, wafer price premiums and lead-time extensions at TSMC N2 become increasingly probable in H2 2026 and into 2027.

**Agentic token multiplier → model routing as mandatory infrastructure:** The 5–30x token consumption multiplier from agentic workflows is not a design flaw enterprises can optimize away — it is structural to multi-step reasoning pipelines. This causally drives model routing (directing traffic to cheapest adequate model) from optional cost-saving measure to mandatory production infrastructure, similar to how CDNs became mandatory once video streaming scaled.

**China certification regime → permanent market bifurcation:** The Anke certification of nine domestic chips formalizes what was previously an informal preference. This is now a compliance requirement, not a preference signal. Western chip vendors (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel) face permanent exclusion from Chinese state procurement — a demand destruction event, not a demand deferral.

**Western-Chinese pricing gap → enterprise compliance re-evaluation:** A 107x price differential is large enough to change compliance calculus. Enterprises that previously avoided Chinese-origin models for data residency reasons will face internal pressure to re-examine which workloads genuinely require that constraint, causing segmentation of AI workflows by sensitivity tier.

**Intel 18A + Terafab anchor customer → Intel Foundry credibility inflection:** Intel Foundry's primary challenge has been the absence of credible external customers at advanced nodes. Terafab represents a $25B committed customer. If 18A yields hold, this causally improves Intel Foundry's ability to attract the next tier of fabless customers who require proof of production-worthy advanced nodes outside TSMC.
