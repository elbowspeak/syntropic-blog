Expect this to drive hyperscalers toward vertical integration (owning more of the chip stack) and enterprises toward on-premise inference for stable workloads.

2. **Nuclear scarcity → data center geography locked in by power access.** As nuclear plants become dedicated AI power sources under long-term agreements, the physical location of future AI compute is being determined *now* by energy asset availability — not by land cost, labor, or latency. 3. **HBM price hikes + AWS spot price rises → accelerator BoM inflation offsets model efficiency gains.** Model efficiency (DeepSeek-style architectural improvements) is reducing *token costs* but is not reducing *hardware acquisition costs*. The supply chain for memory (Samsung, SK Hynix) and GPU capacity is tightening even as software-layer costs fall — meaning efficiency gains accrue to end users and inference providers, not to infrastructure operators.

4. **Gulf sovereign capital + AI infrastructure investment → geopolitical neutralization of compute.** Gulf state AI infrastructure investment is creating a third pole of compute geography (alongside US and China), potentially serving as a neutral zone for non-aligned nations seeking AI access without alignment to either superpower's regulatory or export control regime.
