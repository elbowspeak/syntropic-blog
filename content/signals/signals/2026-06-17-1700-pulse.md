## Causal Updates

**New causal relationship confirmed:** MoE architecture efficiency → structural inference price floor compression → GPU infrastructure demand growth paradox. DeepSeek V3.2 and Kimi K2 demonstrate that activating only a fraction of parameters per forward pass enables large-model capability at commodity pricing. However, this does *not* reduce physical infrastructure demand — it increases total inference volume, which is why GPU cloud prices are rising even as per-token costs fall. The efficiency gain is being consumed by demand expansion, not returned as savings to the infrastructure layer.

**Reinforced relationship:** Rubin's 10x inference cost claim + 3D chip academic results together confirm that the compute efficiency curve is steepening from *both* the architecture side (3D stacking) and the systems integration side (NVLink bandwidth at rack scale). These are compounding, not additive, efficiency vectors.

**New friction introduced:** AFL-CIO resolution creates a labor-permitting dependency in the data center construction supply chain that did not previously exist as an organized political force. This adds a new variable to site selection and construction timeline models.

## Investment Implications

### Increasingly Attractive

**NVIDIA (NVDA)**
Rubin's launch with confirmed hyperscaler adoption and a 10x cost improvement claim over Blackwell reinforces NVIDIA's position as the only vertically integrated AI compute platform with both the silicon and the software ecosystem (CUDA, NVLink) to capture the full rack-level value. The RTX Spark move into client AI is a new revenue vector that was not previously priced. The key risk: if the 3D chip academic work commercializes through a competing foundry, it represents the first credible architectural challenge to NVIDIA's roadmap advantage in several years.

**MediaTek**
The RTX Spark partnership elevates MediaTek from a mid-tier mobile chip supplier to a co-designer of NVIDIA's personal AI compute platform. This is a significant reputational and revenue inflection point. Worth monitoring closely for OEM design win announcements.

**Nuclear Power Operators and SMR Developers**
The convergence of IEA demand projections, direct corporate nuclear contracting (Amazon, Microsoft, Data4/EDF), and the absence of grid alternatives at the required scale makes nuclear power operators structurally undervalued relative to the AI infrastructure buildout. Constellation Energy (operator of the Susquehanna plant), EDF, and emerging SMR developers are direct beneficiaries. The market has partially priced this, but the 12-year contract durations suggest the pricing power extends well beyond current consensus models.

**AI FinOps / Inference Cost Management Software**
The "inference tax" paradox — enterprises seeing total AI costs rise even as per-token costs fall — creates an unserved market for cost attribution, forecasting, and optimization tooling specific to AI workloads. Early-stage companies in this space are likely undervalued given the nascency of the category.

**SkyWater Technology (SKYT)**
Domestic U.S. foundry with validated 3D chip architecture results from a top-tier academic consortium. Geopolitical tailwinds from semiconductor sovereignty mandates. Still early-stage commercially but the strategic positioning is strengthening materially.

### Risk / Pressure

**AMD, Intel, Qualcomm (PC/Edge segment)**
Wall Street's reaction to RTX Spark — share declines across all three — reflects a real structural threat. NVIDIA entering the client AI PC segment with a 1-petaflop, 128GB unified memory chip changes the competitive calculus for NPU-equipped PC chips. This is not a near-term revenue event, but it is a medium-term roadmap threat that requires a credible competitive response.

**Mid-tier Inference API Providers**
With DeepSeek V3.2 at $0.26/million input tokens and SiliconFlow offering 2.3x faster inference at lower cost than major cloud platforms, the market is bifurcating between commodity inference (where Chinese providers and open-source infrastructure dominate on price) and premium inference (where Anthropic and OpenAI compete on capability benchmarks). Mid-tier providers caught between these poles face a squeeze with no clear differentiation moat.

**Enterprises with Undisciplined AI Inference Spend**
The CloudZero data showing average enterprise monthly AI spend approaching $85K+ — with agentic and chained workflows multiplying token consumption non-linearly — suggests material budget exposure at organizations that have not implemented inference cost governance. This is an operational risk, not an investment opportunity, but it is relevant for modeling AI-exposed enterprise software companies' retention and expansion metrics.

### Valuation Disconnect to Monitor

The "inference tax" paradox creates a potential valuation disconnect in hyperscaler cloud businesses: the market is pricing them as infrastructure beneficiaries of AI (correct — capex is surging and being monetized), but the 15% GPU price *increase* alongside 1,000x per-token cost *decrease* suggests that infrastructure pricing power is durable even as model costs commoditize. This is a more bullish setup for AWS, Azure, and GCP than a simple "efficiency reduces cloud spend" narrative would imply — and may be underweighted in current consensus models.
