## Investment Implications

### Structural Winners

**SK Hynix / Micron Technology (HBM)**
HBM is confirmed as the binding constraint on AI GPU shipments — not logic, not fab capacity. Both companies hold near-oligopoly positions in HBM supply. With industry revenue tracking toward $1 trillion and AI accelerator demand compounding, HBM pricing power and allocation control make these names the clearest supply chain leverage plays. The market may be underpricing Micron relative to SK Hynix given Micron's accelerating HBM ramp.

**ASE Technology / Amkor Technology (Advanced Packaging)**
CoWoS-class 2.5D/3D packaging is the second confirmed bottleneck. TSMC's own CoWoS capacity is constrained; third-party advanced packaging providers become overflow and alternative suppliers. These names offer supply chain exposure with less geopolitical concentration risk than pure TSMC plays.

**TSMC**
The cuLitho partnership with Nvidia embeds an AI-driven yield and throughput advantage directly into TSMC's manufacturing process — a moat that is structural, not cyclical. Combined with $1 trillion industry tailwinds and its position as the exclusive or primary foundry for virtually every leading AI chip, TSMC remains the single highest-confidence infrastructure name in the AI supply chain.

**NVIDIA (infrastructure layer, not just chips)**
Beyond GPUs, Nvidia is now embedding itself into the fab operations layer (cuLitho at TSMC), the enterprise inference cloud layer (Vector Core Compute using Blackwell), and the PC market (RTX Spark). The supply chain story is that Nvidia is vertically integrating its influence across compute, manufacturing intelligence, and deployment infrastructure simultaneously. The PC move is a 2027 revenue story but a 2026 competitive moat story.
