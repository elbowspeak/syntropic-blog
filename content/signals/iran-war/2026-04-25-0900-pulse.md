## Situation Summary

The US-Iran conflict remains in a fragile ceasefire phase, with active diplomatic maneuvering now centered on Islamabad as Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi completed talks with Pakistani intermediaries before departing, while US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner travel to Pakistan for indirect negotiations — a framework Iran insists does not constitute direct talks. The IranStrike.com OSINT dashboard recorded **zero kinetic events in the last 24 hours** (against a conflict total of 8,028 events since hostilities began), confirming a continued operational pause on the primary strike front, though the IRGC's seizure of two vessels in the Strait of Hormuz within hours of Trump's ceasefire extension announcement signals that Tehran is actively contesting the maritime domain even during the lull. The US naval blockade continues to tighten its grip economically — with Iran facing an oil storage crisis, 3-4 million estimated job losses, and a nascent stock market reopening under severe structural stress — while Iran's military command issued explicit warnings that continued blockade operations will trigger a military response from its "powerful armed forces." Trajectory assessment: **marginally de-escalating on the kinetic front, re-escalating on the maritime and diplomatic front** — the ceasefire is holding in name, but the architecture underneath it is under active stress from both sides.

The targeting of school textbooks indicates this is regime-level messaging, not fringe rhetoric. Over time, this narrative infrastructure makes future de-escalation harder — it raises the domestic political cost of concessions and locks in a confrontational national identity vis-à-vis the US and Israel for a generation.
