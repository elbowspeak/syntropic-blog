## Situation Summary

Iran's ceasefire collapse (declared "over" by Trump on July 8) has escalated into sustained, multi-front military operations: IranStrike.com logged **31 events in the last 24 hours** (17 strikes, 9 reports, 2 defense actions, 3 movements), with **9 high-severity, 19 medium, 3 low**. The most consequential development is the first confirmed Iranian drone strike on a **US Patriot PAC-3 battery at Erbil Airport** — satellite-confirmed damage to a top-tier American air-defense asset, not just soft infrastructure. Activity spans 11 countries/territories, concentrated in Iran (11 events), Kuwait (4), and Israel (4), with Trump reportedly weighing expansion to nuclear-site strikes and a ground seizure of Kharg Island. **Trajectory: escalating sharply versus the prior 48 hours** — this is now sustained bilateral strike activity plus two simultaneous chokepoint threats (Hormuz, Bab el-Mandeb), not the sporadic tit-for-tat seen immediately post-collapse.

## Strategic Assessment

**Actor intentions (analysis):**
- **Iran** appears to be executing War on the Rocks' three-layer A2/AD doctrine in full simultaneously: degrading forward US basing (Erbil Patriot hit, Kuwait drone attacks), controlling both maritime chokepoints (Hormuz avoidance + Bab el-Mandeb closure threat), and leveraging proxies (Hezbollah resupply push, Houthi activation). The Erbil strike's dual framing — hitting Kurdish separatists *and* the adjacent US base — reflects ISW's observation that Tehran treats external military posture and internal-security suppression as an inseparable "dual-track" effort. *[Analysis, informed by War on the Rocks, ISW]*
- **The US** is using an ultimatum structure — intensified strikes on energy infrastructure as the stick, negotiations as the exit — while reportedly weighing higher-risk, higher-cost options (ground seizure, nuclear strikes) that would break from the campaign's air/missile-only pattern to date. *[Analysis, informed by CIG_telegram reporting + WOTR's "end of managed escalation" thesis]*
- **Israel** continues a largely separate track in Gaza (Khan Younis strikes, yellow-line advance) in parallel with the Iran conflict; the two theaters do not show clear coordination in this 24-hour window, though MEE's report of Israeli troop deployments to Azerbaijan, UAE, Iraq and Somaliland suggests forward-positioning against Iran independent of the Gaza campaign. *[Analysis, informed by IranStrike.com data + MEE]*
- **Gulf states (esp. UAE)** show signs of active co-belligerency rather than passive US basing, per the Bandar Abbas drone-provenance match with MEE's outset-participation report. *[Low-confidence analysis — corroborating but not independently verified]*

**Capability signals (analysis):**
- CSIS assesses the US has expended roughly **50% of THAAD interceptors, 50% of Patriot missiles, and 30% of Tomahawks**, with 3+ years needed to rebuild — a real constraint on sustaining current tempo. *[CSIS]*
- ISW assesses Iran retained **~70% of its pre-war missile inventory** despite the Feb–Apr campaign — suggesting Iran has materially more sustained-strike capacity than a depleted-arsenal narrative would imply. *[ISW]*
- Two Hezbollah-resupply interdictions by Syria in a single day point to an accelerated Iranian rearmament push against active Syrian resistance — worth tracking as a leading indicator of Hezbollah reactivation risk. *[IranStrike.com]*

**Diplomatic dynamics (analysis):**
- Crisis Group's claim of ongoing "technical negotiations" despite Trump's public "it's over" stance suggests the diplomatic track has not fully closed — public escalation rhetoric and private engagement may be running on separate tracks, a pattern common in coercive-diplomacy postures. *[Crisis Group]*
- The June 60-day oil license's current status is unconfirmed in these findings and is a live variable — its revocation or renewal would be a meaningful signal either direction.

**Pattern breaks (analysis):**
- The Erbil Patriot hit is the first confirmed strike on a top-tier US air-defense system in this dataset — a step up from infrastructure/radar targeting.
- Iran threatening **both** Hormuz and Bab el-Mandeb simultaneously is new in scope for this cycle.
- A reported ground-seizure option (Kharg Island) would be the war's first occupation-type action if pursued — currently speculative, not observed.

## Escalation Assessment

**Current level: 4 — Sustained military operations**

Justification: Daily multi-wave strikes are occurring in both directions across at least six countries in 24 hours, including a confirmed hit on a US air-defense battery and Kuwaiti air-defense engagements against Iranian drones. This exceeds "limited military engagements" (3) but does not yet meet "full-scale conflict" (5), since there is no confirmed ground invasion, no confirmed chokepoint closure, and the Kharg Island/nuclear-strike options remain "reportedly considered" rather than executed.

**Change from recent days:** Up from an estimated 3 in the days immediately following the July 8 ceasefire collapse (per War on the Rocks' "Fraying Deals" characterization of sporadic tit-for-tat). The Erbil Patriot hit and the simultaneous two-chokepoint threat mark the shift to sustained, higher-severity operations.

**Would move to 5:** Confirmed US ground action at Kharg Island, US strikes on a nuclear facility, actual (not threatened) closure of Hormuz or Bab el-Mandeb with sustained interdiction, or a mass-casualty strike on a population center.

**Would move down to 3:** Verified halt in cross-border strikes for 48+ hours, confirmed resumption of formal negotiations with a public de-escalatory gesture from either side (e.g., US pausing infrastructure targeting, Iran standing down Gulf-state drone attacks).
