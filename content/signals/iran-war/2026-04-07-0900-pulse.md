## Situation Summary

Today marks one of the most intense escalatory spikes of the six-week conflict, with a massive coordinated rocket and missile barrage targeting Tel Aviv and virtually all of central Israel's population centers executing in near-simultaneous salvos between approximately 15:18–15:42 UTC — the broadest geographic targeting of Israeli civilian infrastructure recorded since conflict onset. IranStrike.com logged **155 events in the past 24 hours** (of 7,190 total since conflict start), including **11 critical-severity** and **50 high-severity** strikes, with 121 events targeting Israel and 27 targeting Iran; the strike-to-report ratio (140 strikes vs. 13 reports) indicates an overwhelmingly kinetic operational tempo. Simultaneously, the US-Israeli air campaign dramatically expanded its Iranian target set, striking railway infrastructure across six provinces, bridges on the Tabriz-Tehran highway, Kharg Island oil export facilities, a Shiraz petrochemical plant tied to ballistic missile precursor production, and a ballistic missile array site in northwestern Iran — with the IDF issuing a nationwide civilian warning to avoid Iranian rail networks for 12 hours. The trajectory is **sharply escalating relative to yesterday**: both the geographic breadth of strikes into Iran and the population-center targeting of Israel represent qualitative escalation steps, not routine operational continuation.
