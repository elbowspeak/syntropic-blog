Diplomatic negotiations to end the 73-day US-Israel war on Iran have effectively collapsed, with both sides publicly accusing the other of bad faith — the most consequential development of the past 24 hours is Trump's publicly signaled consideration of renewing active military operations against Iran, a direct response to Tehran's rejection of the US peace proposal. IranStrike.com recorded **23 events in the last 24 hours** across four target countries (Iran, Israel, Lebanon, UAE), comprising 17 strikes, 3 reports, 2 movement events, and 1 intercept, with severity breakdown of 1 high, 16 medium, and 6 low — activity concentrated heavily in the Israel-Lebanon theater (14 ISR-targeted events, 7 LBN-targeted). The Lebanon front is actively heating up, with Hezbollah killing at least one IDF soldier and eliminating a Radwan Force senior commander in a retaliatory Israeli strike on Dahieh, Beirut — suggesting the Lebanon sub-conflict is escalating in lockstep with the broader Iran diplomatic failure. **Trajectory: Escalating** relative to the past 48 hours — the combination of diplomatic breakdown, renewed US military threat signaling, and active kinetic exchange in Lebanon represents a meaningful deterioration from the fragile ceasefire-and-negotiate posture of the preceding week.

### Actor Intentions

**Iran** is pursuing a negotiating strategy that Crisis Group characterizes as seeking to "renegotiate the rules of de-escalation" rather than capitulate. Today's pattern confirms this: the counter-proposal (Strait sovereignty, full sanctions lift, war damage compensation) is structurally maximalist — designed not to be accepted wholesale, but to anchor a negotiation Tehran hasn't yet agreed to enter on US terms. The domestic execution of Shakourzadeh and the Baha'i crackdown signal the regime is managing internal pressure through coercion rather than concession, suggesting it does not believe itself to be on the verge of political collapse despite severe economic deterioration. *Analysis:* Iran appears to be playing for a negotiated stalemate — not a win — betting that US domestic political fatigue and the failure to achieve nuclear objectives creates diplomatic space over the next several weeks.

**The United States** faces a strategic dilemma crystallized by the nuclear intelligence failure: the war's primary stated objective has not been achieved, the ceasefire is collapsing, and Trump's political brand requires visible results. The "alternative operational designation" language — flagged by abualiexpress and confirmed through IranStrike.com — is analytically interesting: it suggests the US is considering restarting operations in a way that can be packaged as *different* from Epic Fury, potentially to manage legal, Coalition, or domestic political framing. CSIS's assessment that the war is now a "struggle of political endurance" directly maps onto Trump's signal — he is attempting to shift the endurance calculus by threatening costs Iran cannot easily absorb.

**Israel** appears to be using the Lebanon front as an active pressure valve — continuing high-value targeting (Radwan Force command) and absorbing Hezbollah drone strikes while the US-Iran diplomatic track stalls. Netanyahu's CBS hints about US troop deployment suggest Jerusalem is actively lobbying for a more muscular US posture. *Analysis:* Israel's strategic interest is in keeping US military commitment locked in — any diplomatic settlement that leaves Iran's nuclear program intact and its proxy network functional would represent a strategic failure from Jerusalem's perspective. Israeli operations in Lebanon may be calibrated to keep the broader pressure architecture active during the negotiating pause.

**Hezbollah** is demonstrating continued operational capacity — killing an IDF soldier, firing at IAF aircraft, losing a senior Radwan commander — all in a single 24-hour window. This is not a proxy force standing down in deference to a ceasefire; it is one operating with its own escalation logic. *Analysis:* Hezbollah's continuation of lethal operations during the nominal ceasefire period serves two functions: maintaining deterrence credibility with its domestic and regional constituency, and preserving Iran's proxy leverage as a negotiating asset — the implicit threat being that full proxy activation resumes if talks fail.

### Capability Signals

- **Iran nuclear hardening:** ISIS satellite imagery of Pickaxe Mountain showing new defensive measures suggests Iran is adapting its nuclear infrastructure protection posture in real time — potentially making any resumed strike campaign *less* effective against hardened targets than the initial campaign was. *This is a significant capability trend if confirmed.*
- **Hezbollah air engagement capability:** The attempted air-to-ground missile engagement of IAF aircraft is worth flagging as a potential new tactical behavior — if Hezbollah is testing ground-based air engagement of overflying aircraft, this represents an operational escalation in rules of engagement.
- **US strike campaign sustainability:** CSIS documented the US campaign settling at 300–500 targets/day using short-range, lower-cost munitions, suggesting stockpile management has stabilized. However, 73 days of operations at this tempo will have consumed significant precision-guided inventory regardless of unit cost optimization.

### Diplomatic Dynamics

The core structural blockage remains the sequencing dispute identified by Crisis Group: Iran demands blockade-lifting before substantive talks; the US treats the blockade as its primary leverage and will not release it pre-negotiation. Neither side has moved on this structural issue in the past week. Foreign Minister Araghchi's shuttle diplomacy (noted in Crisis Group Monitor #3) indicates Iran still wants third-party mediation — Oman and Qatar remain the most plausible channels — but no mediator has publicly succeeded in bridging the sequencing gap. The UAE's ambiguous position (absorbing Iranian strikes while potentially hosting US forces) makes it an unreliable mediator. *Analysis:* The diplomatic track is not merely stalled; it has structurally regressed — Iran's public maximalist counter-proposal and the US military threat signal represent both sides moving *away* from the April 8 ceasefire framework rather than building on it.

---

### Pattern Breaks

**What happened today that didn't before:** The explicit US consideration of military resumption under a *new operational designation* is genuinely new — it suggests the US is not simply threatening to resume Epic Fury, but is considering a reframed operational concept, which implies planning work has already begun. This is a qualitatively different signal from routine "all options on the table" rhetoric.

**What was expected that didn't happen:** There was no reported Omani or Qatari mediation intervention following the public collapse of talks — diplomatic channels that have historically activated during acute crisis moments appear quiet, which may indicate mediators assessed the gap as currently unbridgeable or are working back-channel without public signal.

## Escalation Assessment

### Current Level: **4 — Sustained Military Operations**

**Justification:** Active kinetic exchange continues on the Lebanon-Israel front with confirmed fatalities on both sides. US military threat of resumption against Iran is now publicly stated. The April 8 ceasefire framework is functionally hollow — Iranian strikes on UAE/Gulf infrastructure as recently as May 4 (Crisis Group Monitor #4) and today's Lebanon exchanges demonstrate that "ceasefire" describes a diplomatic aspiration rather than operational reality. IranStrike.com's 23 events in 24 hours — across 4 countries, with 17 strike-category events — is inconsistent with a genuine cessation of hostilities.

**Change from recent days:** This represents a **deterioration** from the Level 3.5 of the past week (nominal ceasefire holding with occasional violations). The public diplomatic collapse and Trump military signal push the assessment firmly to 4.

**What would move to Level 5:** Confirmed US airstrikes resuming on Iranian soil; Iranian missile barrages on Israel or Gulf states at scale; Hezbollah opening full northern front; Iranian closure of Strait of Hormuz enforced by kinetic action.

**What would move to Level 3:** A credible third-party mediation breakthrough resolving the sequencing dispute; US signaling willingness to partially ease blockade as a confidence-building measure; Iranian withdrawal of the Strait sovereignty demand from its negotiating text.
