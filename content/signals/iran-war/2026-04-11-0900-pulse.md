## Strategic Assessment

### Actor Intentions — What is Each Party Trying to Achieve Right Now?

**Iran:** Tehran's strategy has bifurcated. On the Iran-US axis, Iran is entering negotiations from a deliberately ambiguous posture — publicly stating "conditions are not yet met" while physically sitting across the table from VP Vance. This is textbook Iranian negotiating doctrine: extract maximum concessions before signaling flexibility. Iran's residual leverage — the Hormuz threat, Hezbollah fire on Israel, the cryptocurrency toll demand — are being exercised simultaneously with engagement, not sequentially. The $1/barrel toll demand reveals Iran's economic desperation but also its intent to institutionalize Hormuz control as a revenue stream, not simply a military threat. Iran is trying to survive the ceasefire period economically while Mojtaba Khamenei consolidates domestic authority. *[ANALYSIS — Crisis Group, Iran International, CSIS]*

**Israel:** Israel is pursuing a dual-track strategy that is increasingly in tension with US diplomatic goals. On Iran, Israel achieved its primary objective (Khamenei killed, nuclear infrastructure destroyed, IRGC leadership decimated). On Lebanon, Israel is treating the conflict as unfinished and is explicitly refusing to honor the Lebanon ceasefire that Iran and Pakistan claim exists. The 200+ daily Hezbollah target strikes and building demolitions in southern Lebanese villages suggest Israel is pursuing either a ground operation precursor (clearing the forward zone) or attempting to permanently degrade Hezbollah's border infrastructure during the period when Iran's attention is consumed by Islamabad diplomacy. *[ANALYSIS — IranStrike.com OSINT, CSIS, ISW]*

**United States:** Washington's core objectives in Islamabad appear to be: (1) formalize Strait of Hormuz reopening on terms that allow the US to claim a win, (2) extract some form of Iranian nuclear commitment, and (3) do so before the two-week ceasefire window expires. The presence of VP Vance personally signals the talks are genuinely high-priority, not a diplomatic holding pattern. However, the US faces a structural problem: it cannot simultaneously claim to be negotiating with Iran in good faith while Israel is killing 357 Lebanese civilians per day in a war Iran says is covered by the ceasefire. Washington is managing an alliance tension in real time. *[ANALYSIS — MEE, Al Jazeera, Crisis Group]*

**Hezbollah:** Hezbollah's continued high-tempo fire — particularly the coastal salvo pattern hitting Nahariya and Akhziv Beach — suggests the organization is operating with residual Iranian authorization to maintain pressure as negotiating leverage, while also demonstrating operational continuity despite 200+ IDF strikes daily. The UAV swarms and simultaneous rocket barrages spreading across both the Confrontation Line and Upper Galilee suggest Hezbollah is prioritizing volume and geographic spread over precision, likely reflecting degraded targeting capacity but intact launch infrastructure. *[ANALYSIS — IranStrike.com OSINT, CSIS force assessment]*
