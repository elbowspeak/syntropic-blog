## Situation Summary

The most consequential development in the last 24 hours is the intensification and geographic broadening of the exchange: at 10:20 UTC today, the IRGC launched **simultaneous** ballistic missile/drone strikes on U.S.-linked bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, *and* Saudi Arabia in a single coordinated salvo — a wider single-strike footprint than reported on prior days — while the U.S. continued a systematic campaign to sever Bandar Abbas from its interior via bridge and tunnel strikes, with the day's spotlight event (Shahid Mirzaei Tunnel plus multiple bridges) producing 3 confirmed killed and 8 wounded. IranStrike.com logged **51 events in 24 hours** (38 strikes, 10 reports, 3 movements; severity 29 high / 16 medium / 6 low) spanning **11 countries** — Jordan (12 events) and Iran (11) led, with Iraq (10) and Kuwait (7) close behind, and first same-day appearances of Israel (2) and Syria (1) as locations, which were not prominent targets in comparable prior-day windows. Iran's Health Ministry reported 50 killed and 500 wounded cumulatively over the past three weeks of U.S. strikes, and Al Jazeera reports the seventh consecutive night of U.S. strikes has begun cutting water supply to villages in southern Iran. **Trajectory: escalating** relative to recent days — geographic broadening, multi-country simultaneous IRGC launches, and civilian infrastructure degradation on both sides all point upward.

## Signals

### Strikes | US executes systematic infrastructure-isolation campaign against Bandar Abbas
- **What:** Over the past 24–48 hours the U.S. has struck at least 13+ named bridges, the Shahid Mirzaei Tunnel, and rail/road routes around Bandar Abbas and the Bandar Abbas–Lar corridor in Hormozgan province.
- **So what:** This is not diffuse bombing — it's a deliberate campaign to isolate Iran's principal Strait of Hormuz port hub from IRGC logistics further inland, consistent with a "blockade the blockaders" approach CSIS has described in response to Iran's own Hormuz closure.
- **Confidence:** High (multiple independent Telegram sources, consistent target set)
- **Source:** [CIG_telegram](https://t.me/CIG_telegram/80150), [OSINTdefender](https://t.me/OSINTdefender/19512), [abualiexpress](https://t.me/abualiexpress/126762) via IranStrike.com

### Strikes | IRGC conducts simultaneous triple-country strike on Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia
- **What:** At 10:20 UTC, IRGC ballistic missiles and drones struck U.S.-linked bases in all three Gulf states within the same reporting window, with direct hits confirmed.
- **So what:** Simultaneity across three states in one launch window signals either expanded missile inventory/launch capacity or a deliberate signaling choice to demonstrate multi-front reach — consistent with War on the Rocks' assessment that Iran's asymmetric counterair doctrine targets the "nests and eggs" of U.S. power projection across the entire Gulf basing network rather than concentrating on one target.
- **Confidence:** Medium (single-source cluster, casualty/damage confirmation pending)
- **Source:** [CIG_telegram](https://t.me/CIG_telegram/80111) via IranStrike.com; doctrine context from [War on the Rocks](https://warontherocks.com/irans-asymmetric-counterair-campaign-attacking-the-u-s-air-forces-nests-and-eggs/)

### Strikes | Jordan's Muwaffaq Salti Air Base hit repeatedly, aircraft shelter destroyed
- **What:** At least five separate strike events over 24 hours targeted Muwaffaq Salti Air Base; satellite imagery confirms a direct hit destroying a U.S. aircraft shelter, and NASA firemap data shows a large fire at the base.
- **So what:** Jordan hosting U.S. personnel is now a repeat-strike target rather than a one-off, suggesting Iran assesses it as a lower-retaliation-risk node for sustained pressure compared to direct strikes on Israel or Gulf monarchies with larger U.S. force presence.
- **Confidence:** High (satellite imagery corroboration + multiple Telegram sources)
- **Source:** [CIG_telegram](https://t.me/CIG_telegram/80112), [CIG_telegram](https://t.me/CIG_telegram/80097) via IranStrike.com

### Proxy Forces | Iran strikes Kurdish opposition bases in Sulaymaniyah for third consecutive day
- **What:** Iranian drones and missiles struck weapons depots and Kurdish opposition/Peshmerga-linked facilities in Sulaymaniyah's Tasluja area, marking day three of a sustained campaign against Iranian Kurdish opposition groups based in Iraqi Kurdistan.
- **So what:** This is a distinct axis from the U.S./Gulf exchange — Iran is using the cover of the wider war to settle a long-standing internal-security account against exiled Kurdish opposition groups, which fits the regime's historical pattern of using external conflict windows to act against domestic threats operating from Iraqi territory.
- **Confidence:** High (three consecutive days, multiple corroborating events)
- **Source:** [OSINTdefender](https://t.me/OSINTdefender/19511), [CIG_telegram](https://t.me/CIG_telegram/80076) via IranStrike.com
