**Actor Intentions**

**Iran** is navigating a genuinely unprecedented combination of pressures: physical military defeat (nuclear program severely damaged, navy largely destroyed per CSIS metrics), economic near-collapse (rial at 1.81M/$, sovereign wealth drawdown), leadership decapitation (Khamenei killed February 28 per Crisis Group), and institutional fracture. The Hormuz-for-nuclear-deferral proposal should be read as a *maximum position* — Iran is testing whether it can trade a physical concession it can no longer practically leverage anyway (the Strait is already largely non-functional for Iranian commerce under blockade) for a political concession that preserves the regime's central remaining source of deterrence and international relevance: its nuclear program. The proposal was almost certainly designed to fail in its first form; the expectation of a revised offer within days confirms Iran is still in the game and probing for the acceptable parameter space.

**The United States** under Trump is exhibiting a pattern of maximalist public positioning ("just give up") combined with active diplomatic reception (Trump consulting Rubio and aides on the Iranian proposal). This is consistent with Trump's negotiating style — public maximalism as anchoring, private flexibility — but also carries genuine risk of miscalculation if Tehran interprets the public messaging as a closed door rather than a negotiating posture. CSIS's Byman assessment that "many of the highest costs lie outside the theater" is the key structural constraint on US patience: oil at $4.22+/gallon, European economic disruption, and diplomatic strain from allies are costs that accumulate on the US side even in a "winning" military position.

**Israel** is the actor with the most divergent interests from the US in the current phase. Israeli objectives — permanent disablement of Iran's nuclear program and degradation of the "axis of resistance" — are largely achieved or in progress. Israel has clear incentives to prevent a US-Iran deal that leaves any nuclear enrichment infrastructure intact or that relieves economic pressure on Iran prematurely. The IDF's continued operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon serve Israeli strategic interests regardless of the US-Iran diplomatic track, and Tel Aviv's willingness to keep the Lebanon front active provides a hedge against a US deal that Israel considers insufficiently stringent.

**Hezbollah** is in a structurally weakened but operationally active posture. The escalation in rocket and drone launches, in the context of a "hanging by a thread" ceasefire, looks less like a coordinated Iranian proxy strategy and more like Hezbollah managing its own domestic Lebanese political pressures — demonstrating continued relevance and capability to its constituency — while the broader regional architecture around it deteriorates.
