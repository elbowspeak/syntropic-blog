## Situation Summary

The Iran-Israel-US conflict entered a sharply escalating phase today as Iran launched at least two Sejjil ballistic missiles directly from Shiraz toward Israel — the most consequential Iranian action in recent days — preceded and followed by extensive multi-front rocket and missile salvos targeting northern and central Israel across dozens of localities simultaneously. The 24-hour operational picture is severe: **168 events logged**, with **131 strikes**, **66 high-severity** and **6 critical-severity** incidents, with Israel absorbing 115 of the events and Iran itself registering 39 (reflecting ongoing US/Israeli counter-strikes and a reported US aircraft loss over Iranian territory). A particularly alarming subplot is crystallizing around downed US aircraft: Iranian state media claims to have shot down an American F-35 over central Iran and captured two American pilots, with US search-and-rescue operations confirmed in Khuzestan Province — an event with no precedent in this conflict and with significant escalatory potential. The trajectory is **sharply escalating** relative to yesterday, driven by the direct Iranian ballistic missile launch from Iranian soil, the US aircraft-down crisis, and the geographic breadth of today's salvos.

### Strikes | Iran fires two Sejjil ballistic missiles directly from Shiraz toward Israel

- **What:** At approximately 15:40 UTC, two Sejjil solid-fueled ballistic missiles were launched from the Shiraz area in southern Iran directly targeting Israel, confirmed by both OSINT channel abualiexpress and corroborated by a separate report of two launches from Shiraz at 15:35 UTC.
- **So what:** This matters on multiple levels. The Sejjil is a two-stage solid-fueled missile with a range exceeding 2,000 km and significantly shorter launch preparation time than liquid-fueled systems — its use indicates Iran is still fielding capable ballistic assets despite ISW's March 5 assessment that joint forces had "degraded Iran's number of missile launchers." Launching from Shiraz — deep inside Iranian territory — rather than forward positions suggests Iran is either rotating launch sites to survive suppression, or deliberately demonstrating that its strategic depth remains intact. CSIS notes Iran is pursuing "vertical escalation, ratcheting up through choice of targets, tactics, and weapons" — today's Sejjil launch fits that pattern precisely. Critically, this launch came *after* the Bellingcat-documented US scatterable mine deployment near Shiraz (March 26), suggesting Iran may be responding specifically to that targeting of its Shiraz-area military infrastructure.
- **Confidence:** High

### Strikes | US F-35 reportedly shot down over central Iran; pilots potentially captured

- **What:** Iranian state media and opposition OSINT sources report an American F-35 was downed over central Iran (reports placing the event in the Kohgiluyeh and Boyer-Ahmad / Khuzestan area), with Iranian authorities reporting two American pilots aboard; US search-and-rescue operations are confirmed in Khuzestan Province, and a separate unconfirmed report indicates a US UH-60 Black Hawk conducting SAR operations was also shot down by Iranian air defense.
- **So what:** If confirmed — and the US SAR operation in Iranian territory is a strong corroborating signal — this would represent the single most significant US military loss of the conflict to date, and potentially in decades. It carries compounding strategic consequences: (1) it demonstrates Iran retains functional air defense capable of defeating stealth-optimized aircraft, a major capability signal that reshapes US air campaign planning; (2) American pilots in Iranian custody creates a hostage/leverage dynamic that immediately complicates any diplomacy or ceasefire discussion; (3) it provides Iran with a significant domestic and international propaganda victory. Iran International reports the Kohgiluyeh governor was prioritizing capturing pilots alive — suggesting this is already becoming a deliberate leverage operation. CSIS notes "Iran has ensured that even a tactically successful campaign carries significant strategic liabilities" — this event is precisely that liability materializing.
- **Confidence:** Medium (SAR operations confirmed; F-35 shoot-down and pilot capture are Iranian state media claims pending independent verification; Black Hawk shoot-down unconfirmed)
