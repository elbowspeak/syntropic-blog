## Situation Summary

The conflict entered a new phase of mutual infrastructure targeting today, with the most consequential development being a sustained US/Israeli air campaign against Iran's petrochemical heartland in Mahshahr — with reports of approximately 50+ strikes on facilities including Marun, Amir Kabir, Karoon, Tondgooyan, and Fajr Energy complexes — while Iran simultaneously launched its heaviest rocket salvos of the week against Israeli population centers from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem. IranStrike.com recorded **97 events in the last 24 hours** across a 6,708-event total since conflict start, broken down as: 73 strikes, 4 movements, 4 intercepts, and 16 reports, with severity distribution of 9 critical, 46 high, 37 medium, and 5 low — the highest proportion of high/critical events recorded in recent days. The geographic spread has widened materially, with confirmed or reported strikes touching Israel (59 events), Iran (30), Bahrain (2), Kuwait (2), Iraq (1), Lebanon (2), and the UAE (1), while Iran's selective Strait of Hormuz passage announcement and Trump's April 6 deadline inject an urgent diplomatic tripwire into an already volatile military picture. The overall trajectory is **escalating** relative to yesterday, driven by the shift from military to economic infrastructure targeting on both sides and the approaching presidential deadline.

### Strikes | US/Israel Executes Largest Single-Day Petrochemical Strike Package Against Mahshahr

- **What:** US and/or Israeli warplanes conducted approximately 50+ airstrikes on the Marun Petrochemical Complex in Bandar-e Mahshahr and surrounding facilities — including Amir Kabir, Karoon, Tondgooyan, and Fajr Energy — triggering mass evacuations, heavy black smoke, and a reported power outage across the entire complex.
- **So what:** This represents a deliberate and significant escalation in target set. Previous strikes prioritized military infrastructure, missile production, and leadership targets. Hitting the Mahshahr petrochemical zone — which an Iranian-aligned channel itself assessed as having "greater economic impact than the gas facility attack two and a half weeks ago" (the attack that itself triggered Iranian strikes on Qatari energy infrastructure) — signals a strategic shift toward economic strangulation of the regime. As [CSIS noted](https://www.csis.org/analysis/who-winning-iran-war), Iran's strategy has been to "impose costs and shift the conflict's center of gravity outward" through energy market disruption; the US/Israel appear to be mirroring this logic back against Iran's own petrochemical revenue base. Mahshahr produces feedstocks that supply facilities nationwide — cascading supply disruptions are probable. The Iranian-aligned channel's own assessment of the impact suggests this strike landed where it hurt.

### Strikes | Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant Hit for Third Time; Russian Personnel Evacuated

- **What:** US/Israeli warplanes struck the Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant for a reported third time, killing one security staff member; Russia's Rosatom is evacuating approximately 198 citizens from the facility.
- **So what:** The evacuation of Russian Rosatom personnel is the single most geopolitically significant detail in this event. Russia has thus far refrained from direct military intervention and maintained ambiguous posture toward the conflict. Pulling its technical staff from Bushehr removes Moscow's last significant physical stake inside Iran's nuclear infrastructure — potentially freeing Russia from the diplomatic constraint of protecting its personnel on the ground, but also signaling Russian acknowledgment that the plant is no longer safe or operationally viable. The IAEA confirmed no radiation increase, which limits escalatory pressure from that vector for now. However, [Crisis Group](https://www.crisisgroup.org/cmt/middle-east-north-africa/iran-israelpalestine-united-states/sprawling-middle-east-war-explodes) has flagged that strikes on nuclear infrastructure carry symbolic weight far exceeding their immediate military effect, particularly for audiences in Moscow and Beijing watching the precedent being set. The repeated targeting of Bushehr — even a power-generation facility with civilian purposes — will be weaponized in Iranian and Russian information operations as evidence of Western disregard for nuclear safety norms.
