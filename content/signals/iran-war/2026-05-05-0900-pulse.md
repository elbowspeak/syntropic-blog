Iran has launched its most significant direct attack on Gulf Arab infrastructure to date, striking UAE oil facilities in Fujairah with a salvo of 12 ballistic missiles, 3 cruise missiles, and 4 UAVs — with NASA FIRMS satellite data confirming fires at energy installations despite UAE claims of near-total interception. A second engagement wave was detected just 47 minutes ago, marking back-to-back days of active air defense operations over Emirati territory and confirming that Monday's attack was not a one-off. IranStrike.com recorded **223 events in the past 24 hours** (of 8,264 total since conflict start), with a severity breakdown of **40 high, 1 critical, 165 medium, and 17 low** — and a geographic distribution heavily weighted toward the UAE (127 events), Lebanon (59), and Israel (26). The trajectory is **sharply escalating**: Iran has crossed a previously uncrossed threshold by directly striking a Gulf Cooperation Council state's energy infrastructure, broadening the conflict's geographic perimeter and threatening the GCC's implicit posture of cautious neutrality.
