## Situation Summary

The most consequential development of the past 24 hours is Iraq's direct military strike on Iran's Siri Island floating oil terminal — a dramatic expansion of the conflict's geographic and actor scope that occurred within hours of a Trump-announced Lebanon-Israel ceasefire, suggesting the war is bifurcating rather than winding down. IranStrike.com recorded **52 events in the last 24 hours** (of 7,958 total since conflict start), with a severity breakdown of **7 HIGH, 42 MEDIUM, and 3 LOW** events, and a strike-dominant type profile (44 strikes, 7 reports, 1 movement). The Lebanon-Israel ceasefire entered effect at roughly 21:00 GMT on April 16 but was immediately stressed by more than 7 documented violations within the first hour, while Hezbollah claimed 2,184 attacks over the prior 45-day period. Overall trajectory: **mixed — selectively de-escalating on the Lebanon front while significantly escalating on the Iran-Iraq and Iran energy infrastructure axes.**

### Strikes | Iraq bombs Iran's Siri Island oil terminal in direct retaliatory strike

- **What:** Iraqi warplanes struck Iran's floating oil terminal at Siri Island in the Persian Gulf, hitting the terminal and two to three tankers in retaliation for a prior Iranian missile attack on the Baghdad refinery; satellite imagery confirmed storage tanks severely damaged and on fire.
- **So what:** This is a structural escalation event, not a skirmish. Iraq launching offensive airstrikes against Iranian sovereign energy infrastructure crosses a threshold that has not been crossed in this conflict — it means a second state actor is now conducting independent kinetic operations against Iran's economic lifeline. Siri Island is one of Iran's primary offshore crude loading facilities; damage there directly compounds the economic strangulation already imposed by the US naval blockade. Critically, the strike was launched *hours after* Trump announced a Lebanon ceasefire, which may suggest Baghdad calculated that US diplomatic attention was elsewhere, or alternatively that Washington gave a permissive signal. CSIS analysis has noted that Iran's strategy has been to "impose costs outward" to shift the conflict's center of gravity — this Iraqi strike suggests that strategy is generating blowback Iran did not fully anticipate: regional states are now retaliating independently rather than deferring to US-led frameworks. The unclaimed status of the earlier satellite-confirmed damage also raises the possibility of a prior, separate strike actor — potentially Israel operating covertly before Iraq's acknowledged strike.
- **Confidence:** High (corroborated by OSINTdefender Telegram and satellite imagery)
- **Source:** [OSINTdefender via IranStrike.com](https://t.me/OSINTdefender/18644)
