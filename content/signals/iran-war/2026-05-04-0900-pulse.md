### Actor Intentions

**Iran** is executing a coherent, if high-risk, dual-track strategy: submitting a maximalist peace proposal while simultaneously maintaining sub-threshold coercive pressure (Hormuz harassment, Hezbollah proxy activity) to demonstrate the cost of rejection. The proposal's deliberate decoupling of war termination from nuclear issues reveals that Tehran's strategic priority is *relief from economic and military pressure*, not genuine engagement on the nuclear file. The regime is betting that Trump's transactional instincts and desire for a "deal" can be exploited — offering him the optics of a Hormuz reopening and war's end without the substance of nuclear rollback. This is consistent with [War on the Rocks'](https://warontherocks.com/2026/02/for-irans-regime-better-to-take-a-beating-than-capitulate/) assessment that "giving in to U.S. pressure could weaken [Khamenei's] hold at home" — the nuclear deferral is not a negotiating position; it is a political survival requirement.

**The United States** appears internally divided. Trump's simultaneous announcement of a naval escort mission and his review of Iran's 14-point proposal suggests competing impulses: deal-making instinct versus hawkish pressure from advisers (Rubio reportedly in consultations). The naval escort mission is operationally significant — it puts US forces in direct proximity to IRGC harassing craft and materially raises the risk of a kinetic incident that could shatter the ceasefire. The decision to publicize this mission rather than conduct it quietly suggests it is at least partly a coercive signal to Tehran: *the blockade enforces itself.*

**Israel** is the actor most exposed by any US-Iran deal that defers nuclear issues. Israel's continued Lebanon strikes during the ceasefire period serve multiple functions: maintaining military pressure on Hezbollah, signaling to Washington that the Iran threat system is not neutralized, and preserving domestic political legitimacy for the Netanyahu government. Israel has strong incentive to torpedo any framework that leaves Iran's nuclear posture intact — expect Israeli pressure on Washington to harden nuclear preconditions.

**Hezbollah** is operating as an extension of Iranian negotiating leverage, not as an independent actor in this phase. The rocket-and-drone campaign during an extended truce is calibrated — enough to sustain pressure and demonstrate Iranian proxy resilience, not enough to trigger a full IDF ground operation that would remove the Lebanon card from Iran's hand.

The 65-day internet shutdown in Iran is an underappreciated capability signal: it reflects the regime's judgment that domestic communication networks represent a greater near-term threat to stability than the reputational cost of the shutdown.
