## Landscape Assessment

The ecosystem is in a **deployment-outpacing-governance** phase, not a capability breakthrough phase. Nothing this week represents a fundamental leap in what agents can do — the model releases (Sonnet 5, GPT-5.6, Grok 4.5, Muse Spark 1.1) are incremental, and the benchmark story (SWE-Bench's validity crisis) actively cuts against the narrative of frontier capability gains. What's real and shipping is **infrastructure hardening**: MCP's move to a stateless, governed 2.0 spec; Claude Agent SDK's new guardrails against runaway loops and delegation; enterprise-managed MCP access via Okta. This is the plumbing layer catching up to adoption that already happened. The five-nation government guidance and Deloitte's governance-maturity finding (1 in 5) are the clearest tell: regulators and enterprises are reacting to agents already being widely deployed with weak safety rails, not preparing for a future rollout.
