## Landscape Assessment

The AI agent ecosystem in late March 2026 has crossed a visible inflection from experimentation to infrastructure buildout. Three concurrent dynamics define this week's picture:

**Protocol standardization is effectively complete at the MCP layer.** With Linux Foundation governance, four official language SDKs, 100+ production servers, and competing frameworks (Microsoft, Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, CrewAI) all implementing MCP and A2A, the protocol wars are over. The battleground has shifted upward to the gateway and security layer — where the money is now moving — and downward to runtime performance and developer experience.

**The framework layer is consolidating around four credible enterprise options.** LangGraph (proven, compliance-friendly, battle-tested at Uber/LinkedIn/Klarna), Microsoft Agent Framework (newly consolidated, Azure-native, GA imminent), Google ADK (Gemini-optimized, Vertex-integrated), and Anthropic's Claude Agent SDK (model-native, strong safety properties) are the serious enterprise contenders. CrewAI remains the high-velocity open-source option for teams that want rapid prototyping and broad community tooling. AutoGen's move to maintenance mode is the week's clearest consolidation signal.

**The production deployment evidence is real but narrowly distributed.** Stripe's 1,300+ PRs/week, Cognition's $73M ARR, GitHub's 55% developer speed improvement, and the named enterprise deployments at Uber, LinkedIn, and Klarna are genuine traction. But SWE-Bench Pro's sub-24% enterprise performance figures and the benchmark reliability findings are a counterweight — the gap between demo performance and production performance on real enterprise codebases remains large. The companies winning in production share a common pattern: human review gates, explicit exit criteria, structured JSON handoffs between agents, and tiered autonomy by risk level. The "fully autonomous, no human in loop" narrative is not what is actually shipping.

The $3.6B AIDR funding wave during RSAC is the week's most significant macro signal — it means institutional capital has concluded that agentic security is a durable, large-market problem, not a transitional concern that foundation model providers will solve internally.
