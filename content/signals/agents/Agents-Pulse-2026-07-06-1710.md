## Landscape Assessment

The past week's findings (viewed against the Q2 2026 backdrop) confirm a market in **consolidation and hardening**, not a market in early experimentation. Three converging trends stand out:

1. **Platform convergence at the top**: Microsoft's forced merger of AutoGen/Semantic Kernel into Agent Framework, and LangChain's rebrand of LangGraph Platform into LangSmith Deployment, both signal that vendors are done fragmenting their own product lines — they are consolidating build/deploy/observe into single branded surfaces to compete for enterprise platform lock-in.

2. **Protocols are stabilizing, not proliferating**: MCP's move to Linux Foundation governance plus its transport standardization (Streamable HTTP), and A2A's 150+ org adoption, suggest the interoperability layer war is resolving toward two complementary standards rather than a dozen incompatible ones. This is a maturity signal typically seen 18-24 months into a technology's production lifecycle — arriving faster here.

3. **The gap between agent hype and hardened production infrastructure is closing, but unevenly**: OpenAI shipping Sandbox Agents and Secure MCP Tunnels, and Goldman Sachs naming Devin explicitly as workforce infrastructure, show real production deployment. But OpenAI simultaneously killing Agent Builder/Evals within the same quarter it launched shows that even leading vendors are still finding product-market fit for the *tooling layer* around agents, even as the *protocol layer* (MCP/A2A) and *coding agent* use case mature faster.

The net picture: infrastructure and protocols are maturing rapidly; enterprise-facing "no-code" agent-building tools are still unstable; coding agents (Devin) and framework-level primitives (durable state, subagents, sandboxing) are the areas showing the most genuine deployment traction.

## Predictions

- **MCP becomes the de facto default**, similar to how REST/HTTP became default for web APIs — expect more cloud platforms (beyond Salesforce, Cloudflare, Replit, Azure) to announce native MCP support within two quarters, and expect vendor-specific "connector" ecosystems to shrink in favor of MCP server registries.
- **Framework count will shrink, not grow** — expect further M&A or "maintenance mode" announcements among mid-tier orchestration frameworks as Microsoft, LangChain, and Anthropic's Claude Agent SDK absorb feature parity and mindshare. Frameworks without a hyperscaler or major-lab backer will struggle to justify independent existence.
- **Coding agents will be the first mass enterprise beachhead** for autonomous (not just copilot) agents — Devin's Goldman Sachs deployment plus the "hybrid workforce" framing suggests other regulated enterprises (banks, insurers) will follow with named pilots in the next 2-3 quarters, likely with human-in-the-loop review gates (echoing Devin's "Diff Line Permalinks" and desktop QA review features).
- **Expect continued churn in the "low-code agent builder" category** — OpenAI's Agent Builder/Evals wind-down is likely a preview of similar consolidation at other vendors as the market realizes that visual workflow builders are a weaker moat than protocol/runtime/observability layers.
- **Observability will bifurcate** into "evaluation-first" platforms (Braintrust) versus "open-source self-hosted" (Langfuse, Phoenix) — expect enterprise buyers to demand causal tracing (tool call → root cause) as a baseline procurement requirement within the year.
