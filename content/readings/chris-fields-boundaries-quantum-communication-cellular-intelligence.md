Fields' career arc (nuclear physics → genomics → quantum information theory) converges on one question: how does system identification work? Working with Levin and Friston, he treats biological systems as information processors with goals and predictions. The boundary is where all the action is — what a system can know about its environment is exactly what's encoded on the boundary between them. This is the Markov blanket, the holographic principle, the computer interface — all the same idea.
